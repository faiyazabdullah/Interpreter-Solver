### Step-by-Step Solution:

#### **Step 1: Analyze the Given Information**
The image depicts a composite solid consisting of two hemispheres:
- The larger hemisphere has a diameter of \(12\) cm.
- The smaller hemisphere has a diameter of \(6\) cm.

We need to find the total volume of the composite solid.

#### **Step 2: Recall the Formula for the Volume of a Hemisphere**
The volume \(V\) of a hemisphere is given by:
\[
V_{\text{hemisphere}} = \frac{2}{3} \pi r^3
\]
where \(r\) is the radius of the hemisphere.

#### **Step 3: Calculate the Radius of Each Hemisphere**
- For the larger hemisphere:
  \[
  \text{Diameter} = 12 \implies \text{Radius} = \frac{12}{2} = 6 \text{ cm}
  \]
- For the smaller hemisphere:
  \[
  \text{Diameter} = 6 \implies \text{Radius} = \frac{6}{2} = 3 \text{ cm}
  \]

#### **Step 4: Calculate the Volume of Each Hemisphere**
Using the formula \(V_{\text{hemisphere}} = \frac{2}{3} \pi r^3\):

- **Volume of the larger hemisphere:**
  \[
  V_{\text{large}} = \frac{2}{3} \pi (6)^3 = \frac{2}{3} \pi (216) = \frac{432}{3} \pi = 144 \pi \text{ cm}^3
  \]

- **Volume of the smaller hemisphere:**
  \[
  V_{\text{small}} = \frac{2}{3} \pi (3)^3 = \frac{2}{3} \pi (27) = \frac{54}{3} \pi = 18 \pi \text{ cm}^3
  \]

#### **Step 5: Calculate the Total Volume of the Composite Solid**
The total volume \(V_{\text{total}}\) is the sum of the volumes of the two hemispheres:
\[
V_{\text{total}} = V_{\text{large}} + V_{\text{small}} = 144 \pi + 18 \pi = 162 \pi \text{ cm}^3
\]

#### **Step 6: Approximate the Volume Using \(\pi \approx 3.1416\)** 
\[
V_{\text{total}} = 162 \pi \approx 162 \times 3.1416 = 508.9392 \text{ cm}^3
\]

#### **Step 7: Round the Final Answer to Two Decimal Places**
\[
V_{\text{total}} \approx 508.94 \text{ cm}^3
\]

### Final Answer:
\[
\boxed{508.94 \text{ cm}^3}
\]
