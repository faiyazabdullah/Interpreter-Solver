#### 2. **Understand the Problem:**
   We need to find the ratio of the volume of Sphere A to Sphere B. The formula for the volume of a sphere is:
   \[
   V = \frac{4}{3} \pi r^3
   \]
   where \( r \) is the radius of the sphere.

#### 3. **Calculate the Volume of Each Sphere:**
   - **Volume of Sphere A (\( V_A \)):**
     \[
     V_A = \frac{4}{3} \pi r_A^3 = \frac{4}{3} \pi (3)^3 = \frac{4}{3} \pi (27) = 36 \pi

     V_B = \frac{4}{3} \pi r_B^3 = \frac{4}{3} \pi (12)^3 = \frac{4}{3} \pi (1728) = 2304 \pi

#### 4. **Find the Ratio of the Volumes:**
   The ratio of the volumes of two similar spheres is the cube of the ratio of their radii. Mathematically:
   \[
   \text{Ratio of volumes} = \left( \frac{r_A}{r_B} \right)^3
   \]

   \[
   \frac{r_A}{r_B} = \frac{3}{12} = \frac{1}{4}

   \[
   \left( \frac{r_A}{r_B} \right)^3 = \left( \frac{1}{4} \right)^3 = \frac{1^3}{4^3} = \frac{1}{64}
