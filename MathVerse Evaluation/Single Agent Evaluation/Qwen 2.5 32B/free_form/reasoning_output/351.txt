QUESTION:
A man stands at point A looking at the top of two poles. Pole 1 has height 8 m and angle of elevation 34^\circ  and Pole 2 has height 25 m and angle of elevation 57^\circ . The man wishes to find the distance between the two poles.
Hence find BC, the distance between the two poles in metres.

Round your answer to one decimal place.

### Problem Analysis:
We are tasked with finding the distance \( BC \) between two poles, given the following information:
- Pole 1 has a height of \( 8 \) meters and an angle of elevation of \( 34^\circ \).
- Pole 2 has a height of \( 25 \) meters and an angle of elevation of \( 57^\circ \).
- The observer is at point \( A \), and we need to determine the distance \( BC \).

### Step-by-Step Solution:

#### **Step 1: Define Variables and Known Information**
- Let \( AB = x \): the horizontal distance from the observer \( A \) to the base of Pole 1.
- Let \( AC = y \): the total horizontal distance from the observer \( A \) to the base of Pole 2.
- We need to find \( BC = y - x \).

#### **Step 2: Use Trigonometry for Pole 1**
For Pole 1:
- The height of Pole 1 is \( 8 \) meters.
- The angle of elevation from \( A \) to the top of Pole 1 is \( 34^\circ \).

Using the tangent function:
\[
\tan(34^\circ) = \frac{\text{opposite}}{\text{adjacent}} = \frac{8}{x}
\]
\[
x = \frac{8}{\tan(34^\circ)}
\]

Calculate \( \tan(34^\circ) \):
\[
\tan(34^\circ) \approx 0.6745
\]
\[
x = \frac{8}{0.6745} \approx 11.86 \text{ meters}
\]

#### **Step 3: Use Trigonometry for Pole 2**
For Pole 2:
- The height of Pole 2 is \( 25 \) meters.
- The angle of elevation from \( A \) to the top of Pole 2 is \( 57^\circ \).

Using the tangent function:
\[
\tan(57^\circ) = \frac{\text{opposite}}{\text{adjacent}} = \frac{25}{y}
\]
\[
y = \frac{25}{\tan(57^\circ)}
\]

Calculate \( \tan(57^\circ) \):
\[
\tan(57^\circ) \approx 1.5399
\]
\[
y = \frac{25}{1.5399} \approx 16.24 \text{ meters}
\]

#### **Step 4: Calculate \( BC \)**
The distance \( BC \) is the difference between \( y \) and \( x \):
\[
BC = y - x
\]
Substitute the values of \( y \) and \( x \):
\[
BC = 16.24 - 11.86 \approx 4.38 \text{ meters}
\]

#### **Step 5: Round to One Decimal Place**
Rounding \( 4.38 \) to one decimal place gives:
\[
BC \approx 4.4 \text{ meters}
\]

### Final Answer:
\[
\boxed{4.4}
