### Step-by-Step Solution:

#### Step 1: Recall the formula for the volume of a pyramid
The volume \( V \) of a pyramid is given by:
\[
V = \frac{1}{3} \times \text{Base Area} \times \text{Height}
\]
Here:
- The base area (\( A \)) is the area of the square base.
- The height (\( h \)) is the perpendicular distance from the apex of the pyramid to the center of the base.

#### Step 2: Calculate the base area
The base of the pyramid is a square with side length \( s = 10 \, \text{cm} \). The area of a square is given by:
\[
\text{Base Area} = s^2 = 10^2 = 100 \, \text{cm}^2
\]

#### Step 3: Substitute the values into the volume formula
Now, substitute the base area and the height into the volume formula:
\[
V = \frac{1}{3} \times \text{Base Area} \times \text{Height}
\]
\[
V = \frac{1}{3} \times 100 \, \text{cm}^2 \times 6 \, \text{cm}
\]

#### Step 4: Perform the calculation
First, multiply the base area by the height:
\[
100 \, \text{cm}^2 \times 6 \, \text{cm} = 600 \, \text{cm}^3
\]
Next, divide by 3:
\[
V = \frac{1}{3} \times 600 \, \text{cm}^3 = 200 \, \text{cm}^3
\]
