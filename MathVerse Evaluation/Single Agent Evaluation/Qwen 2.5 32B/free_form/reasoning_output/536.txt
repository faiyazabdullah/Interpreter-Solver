### Problem Analysis:
We are given the graph of a trigonometric function with specific points marked:
- Minimum point: $\left(-\frac{1}{2} \pi, 2.4\right)$
- Maximum point: $\left(\frac{3}{2} \pi, 13\right)$

The task is to determine the amplitude of the function. The amplitude of a trigonometric function is defined as half the distance between its maximum and minimum values.

### Step-by-Step Solution:

#### 1. Identify the Minimum and Maximum Values:
From the given points:
- The minimum value of the function is $y = 2.4$.
- The maximum value of the function is $y = 13$.

#### 2. Calculate the Distance Between the Maximum and Minimum Values:
The vertical distance between the maximum and minimum values is:
\[
\text{Distance} = \text{Maximum value} - \text{Minimum value} = 13 - 2.4 = 10.6
\]

#### 3. Determine the Amplitude:
The amplitude is half of this distance:
\[
\text{Amplitude} = \frac{\text{Distance}}{2} = \frac{10.6}{2} = 5.3
\]
