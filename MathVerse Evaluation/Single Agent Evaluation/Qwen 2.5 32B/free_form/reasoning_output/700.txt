We are tasked with finding the surface area of the rectangular prism.

### Step-by-Step Solution:

#### **Step 1: Recall the formula for the surface area of a rectangular prism**
The surface area \( S \) of a rectangular prism is given by:
\[
S = 2(lw + lh + wh)
\]
where:
- \( l \) is the length,
- \( w \) is the width,
- \( h \) is the height.

#### **Step 2: Substitute the given dimensions into the formula**
From the problem:
- \( l = 12 \) m,
- \( w = 6 \) m,
- \( h = 4 \) m.

Substitute these values into the formula:
\[
S = 2((12)(6) + (12)(4) + (6)(4))
\]

#### **Step 3: Calculate each term inside the parentheses**
1. \( lw = 12 \times 6 = 72 \)
2. \( lh = 12 \times 4 = 48 \)
3. \( wh = 6 \times 4 = 24 \)

So, the expression becomes:
\[
S = 2(72 + 48 + 24)
\]

#### **Step 4: Simplify the terms inside the parentheses**
Add the values:
\[
72 + 48 + 24 = 144
\]

Now, substitute back:
\[
S = 2(144)
\]

#### **Step 5: Multiply to find the surface area**
\[
S = 2 \times 144 = 288
\]

#### **Step 6: Include the units**
The surface area is in square meters (\( \text{m}^2 \)).

### Final Answer:
\[
\boxed{288 \text{ m}^2}
