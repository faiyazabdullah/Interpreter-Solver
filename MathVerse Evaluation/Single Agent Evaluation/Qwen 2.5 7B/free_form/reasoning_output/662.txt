   The lateral surface area \(A_{\text{lateral}}\) of a cylindrical shell is given by:
   \[
   A_{\text{lateral}} = 2\pi r h
   \]
   where \(r\) is the average radius of the cylindrical shell and \(h\) is the height (or length) of the cylinder.

   The outer radius \(R\) is 11 cm, and the inner radius \(r\) is 10 cm. The average radius \(r_{\text{avg}}\) is:
   \[
   r_{\text{avg}} = \frac{R + r}{2} = \frac{11 + 10}{2} = 10.5 \text{ cm}

   The height \(h\) is given as 24 cm.

   Therefore, the lateral surface area is:
   \[
   A_{\text{lateral}} = 2\pi \times 10.5 \times 24 = 504\pi \text{ cm}^2
   \]

2. **Calculate the areas of the two circular bases**:
   The area of one circular base is given by:
   \[
   A_{\text{base}} = \pi R^2 \quad \text{for the outer base}

   \[

   A_{\text{inner base}} = \pi \times 10^2 = 100\pi \text{ cm}^2
   \]

   The total area of the two bases is:
   \[
   A_{\text{bases}} = A_{\text{outer base}} + A_{\text{inner base}} = 121\pi + 100\pi = 221\pi \text{ cm}^2
   \]

3. **Sum the lateral surface area and the areas of the two bases**:

   \text{Total Surface Area} = A_{\text{lateral}} + A_{\text{bases}} = 504\pi + 221\pi = 725\pi \text{ cm}^2
