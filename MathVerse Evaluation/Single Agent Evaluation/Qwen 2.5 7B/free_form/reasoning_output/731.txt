To find the volume of the prism, we use the formula for the volume of a prism:

\[ \text{Volume} = \text{Base Area} \times \text{Height} \]

Given:
- The base of the prism is a rectangle with width \(4 \text{ cm}\) and height \(8 \text{ cm}\).
- The height of the prism is \(2 \text{ cm}\).

First, calculate the area of the base:
\[ \text{Base Area} = \text{width} \times \text{height} = 4 \text{ cm} \times 8 \text{ cm} = 32 \text{ cm}^2 \]

Next, multiply the base area by the height of the prism:
\[ \text{Volume} = 32 \text{ cm}^2 \times 2 \text{ cm} = 64 \text{ cm}^3 \]
