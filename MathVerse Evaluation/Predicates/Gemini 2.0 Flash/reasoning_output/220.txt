================================================================================
Question: In \odot G, CE and AB are diameters. \angle A G D is a right angle and \angle A G C is 60 degrees. Find m \angle B G E.
================================================================================

STEP 1: COMPREHENSIVE IMAGE ANALYSIS
- Shapes: Circle, lines, angles
- Points: A, B, C, D, E, G
- Lines: AB, CE, GD, GE, GA, GC
- Angles: <AGC = 60, <AGD = 90
- Markings: Right angle at <AGD, radii appear equal
- Implied: G is the center of the circle, AB and CE are diameters, all radii are equal.

STEP 2: CIRCLE-SPECIFIC ANALYSIS
- Center: G
- Points on Circle: A, B, C, D, E
- Radii: GA, GB, GC, GD, GE
- Diameters: AB, CE
- No inscribed angles are immediately visible.
- Central angles: AGC, AGD, BGE, etc.
- Radii are equal: GA = GB = GC = GD = GE

STEP 3: ANGLE AND PERPENDICULARITY ANALYSIS
- <AGC = 60 degrees
- <AGD = 90 degrees (right angle)
- <DGE + <EGB = <DGB
- <AGB is a straight angle = 180 degrees
- <CGE is a straight angle = 180 degrees
- <AGD is a right angle. Therefore, GD is perpendicular to AB.

STEP 4: CONGRUENCE AND EQUALITY ANALYSIS
- GA = GB = GC = GD = GE (radii)
- Vertical angles: <AGC and <BGE are vertical angles (but we don't know if they are equal yet)

STEP 5: INTERSECTION AND POSITIONING ANALYSIS
- AB and CE intersect at G.
- D and E lie on the circle.
- A, G, B are collinear
- C, G, E are collinear

STEP 6: CONSTRAINT AND RELATIONSHIP SYNTHESIS
- Since AB is a diameter, <AGB = 180. Similarly, <CGE = 180.
- <CGA + <AGD + <DGB = 180. Since <CGA = 60 and <AGD = 90, <DGB = 180 - 60 - 90 = 30.
- <DGE + <EGB = <DGB = 30
- <AGC and <BGE are vertical angles, so <AGC = <BGE = 60

STEP 7: QUESTION-DRIVEN COMPLETENESS CHECK
- We have identified all the key angles, radii, and relationships. We can solve for <BGE with the given information.
