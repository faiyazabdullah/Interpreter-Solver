Question: As shown in the figure, the diameter CD of circle O crosses the midpoint G of chord EF, angle DCF = 20.0, then angle EOD is equal to ()
================================================================================

STEP 1: COMPREHENSIVE IMAGE ANALYSIS
- Circle O is present.
- Points: C, D, E, F, G, O
- Lines: CD, EF, OC, OF, OE
- Angle DCF = 20 degrees.
- CD is the diameter of circle O.
- G is the midpoint of chord EF.
- CD is perpendicular to EF.

STEP 2: CIRCLE-SPECIFIC ANALYSIS
- O is the center of the circle.
- C, D, E, F are points on the circle.
- OC, OD, OE, OF are radii.
- CD is a diameter.
- EF is a chord.
- Since G is the midpoint of EF, and G lies on CD, then CD is perpendicular to EF.
- OE = OF = OC = OD (all radii are equal)

STEP 3: ANGLE AND PERPENDICULARITY ANALYSIS
- Angle DCF = 20 degrees.
- Angle CGF = 90 degrees.
- Angle DGE = 90 degrees.

STEP 4: CONGRUENCE AND EQUALITY ANALYSIS
- OE = OF (radii of the same circle)

STEP 5: INTERSECTION AND POSITIONING ANALYSIS
- CD intersects EF at G.
- O lies on CD.
- G lies on CD and EF.

STEP 6: CONSTRAINT AND RELATIONSHIP SYNTHESIS
- Triangle OEF is isosceles.
- Since G is the midpoint of EF, OG is perpendicular to EF. Therefore, CD is perpendicular to EF.
- Angle DCF = 20.  Then Angle COF = 2 * angle CEF.  Also, Angle EOF = 2 * angle ECF

STEP 7: QUESTION-DRIVEN COMPLETENESS CHECK
- We need to determine angle EOD.
- We know angle DCF = 20.
- We know CD is perpendicular to EF.
- We need to relate angle DCF to angle EOD.
