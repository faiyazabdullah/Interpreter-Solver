Question: As shown in the figure, angle C = 36.0, rotate triangle ABC anticlockwise around point A by 60.0 to get triangle AED, AD and BC intersect at point F, then the degree of angle AFC is ()
================================================================================

STEP 1: COMPREHENSIVE IMAGE ANALYSIS
- Shapes: Triangle ABC, Triangle AED, lines AD, BC, AE, AB, DE, AC. Point F is the intersection of AD and BC.
- Measurements: Angle C = 36 degrees, Angle EAB = 60 degrees (rotation).
- Implied: Rotation means AC = AE, AB = AD, and Angle CAE = Angle DAB = 60 degrees.

STEP 2: ANGLE AND PERPENDICULARITY ANALYSIS
- Angle C = 36 degrees
- Angle EAB = 60 degrees
- Since triangle ABC is rotated to triangle AED around A, Angle CAB = Angle EAD, and Angle ABC = Angle ADE, Angle ACB = Angle AED
- Angle CAE = 60 degrees, Angle DAB = 60 degrees.
- In triangle AFC, we need to find Angle AFC.

STEP 3: CONGRUENCE AND EQUALITY ANALYSIS
- Triangle ABC is congruent to Triangle AED (rotation).
- AC = AE, AB = AD, BC = DE

STEP 4: INTERSECTION AND POSITIONING ANALYSIS
- AD and BC intersect at F.

STEP 5: CONSTRAINT AND RELATIONSHIP SYNTHESIS
- We can find Angle CAB from the angles in triangle ABC. Angle CAB = 180 - Angle ABC - Angle C. Let Angle ABC = x. Then Angle CAB = 180 - x - 36 = 144 - x.
- Angle DAF = Angle DAB - Angle FAB = 60 - Angle FAB.
- In triangle AFB, Angle AFB = 180 - Angle FAB - Angle ABF = 180 - Angle FAB - x.
- Angle AFC = 180 - Angle AFB = 180 - (180 - Angle FAB - x) = Angle FAB + x.
- Angle BAC = Angle EAD. Angle BAC = 144 - x. Angle CAD = Angle BAC - Angle BAD = 144 - x - Angle BAD
- Angle CAD = Angle EAD - Angle EAC
- Angle CAD = 144 - x - 60 = 84 - x
