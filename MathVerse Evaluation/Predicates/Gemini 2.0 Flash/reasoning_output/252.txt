Question: In triangle A B C, A D, B E, and C F intersect at point Q. Q is the centroid and B E = 9. Find B Q.
================================================================================

- Geometric shapes: Triangle ABC, Lines AD, BE, CF
- Points: A, B, C, D, E, F, Q
- Measurements: BE = 9
- Markings: Q is the centroid of triangle ABC. AD, BE, and CF are medians.

STEP 2: CIRCLE-SPECIFIC ANALYSIS
- No circles are present.

STEP 3: ANGLE AND PERPENDICULARITY ANALYSIS
- No right angles are explicitly marked.

STEP 4: CONGRUENCE AND EQUALITY ANALYSIS
- Since Q is the centroid, AD, BE, and CF are medians.

STEP 5: INTERSECTION AND POSITIONING ANALYSIS
- AD, BE, and CF intersect at point Q.
- D is the midpoint of BC.
- E is the midpoint of AC.
- F is the midpoint of AB.
- Q is the centroid of the triangle.
- Since Q is the centroid, BQ = (2/3)BE.

STEP 6: CONSTRAINT AND RELATIONSHIP SYNTHESIS
- The centroid divides each median in a 2:1 ratio. Therefore, BQ = 2*QE and QE = (1/3)BE.
- Given BE = 9, BQ = (2/3)*9 = 6.
