================================================================================
PROBLEM 394 - STEP-BY-STEP REASONING
================================================================================
Question: The radius of the following figure is $2 \mathrm{~cm}$. Calculate the area of the following figure, correct to one decimal place.
================================================================================

STEP 1: COMPREHENSIVE IMAGE ANALYSIS
- Shape: A sector of a circle. Let the center be O, and the endpoints of the arc be A and B.
- Lines: Two radii: OA and OB
- Angle: Angle AOB is a right angle (90 degrees).
- Measurement: Radius = 2 cm.
- Right angle marking is present.

STEP 2: CIRCLE-SPECIFIC ANALYSIS
- Center: Assumed to be O. Points A and B lie on the circle.
- OA and OB are radii.
- Angle AOB is a central angle.

STEP 3: ANGLE AND PERPENDICULARITY ANALYSIS
- Angle AOB is a right angle. Therefore, OA is perpendicular to OB.

STEP 4: CONGRUENCE AND EQUALITY ANALYSIS
- OA = OB = radius.

STEP 5: INTERSECTION AND POSITIONING ANALYSIS
- OA and OB intersect at O.

STEP 6: CONSTRAINT AND RELATIONSHIP SYNTHESIS
- The figure is a sector with a central angle of 90 degrees. The area of the sector is 1/4 of the entire circle area.

STEP 7: QUESTION-DRIVEN COMPLETENESS CHECK
- All necessary information for area calculation is present: radius and right angle.
