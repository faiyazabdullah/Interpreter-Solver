PROBLEM 204 - STEP-BY-STEP REASONING
================================================================================
Question: Two triangles X N M and X Z Y are similar with a shared vertex X. M N and Y Z are parallel. If X N = 6, X M = 2, and X Y = 10, find N Z.
================================================================================

STEP 1: COMPREHENSIVE IMAGE ANALYSIS
- Geometric shapes: Triangle, Lines, Points
- Points: X, Y, Z, M, N
- Lines: XY, XZ, YZ, XM, XN, MN
- Measurements: XN = 6, XM = 2, XY = 10, MY = 8 (10-2), NZ = $
- Parallel lines: MN || YZ.
- Similar Triangles: Triangle(XNM) and Triangle(XYZ) are similar.

STEP 2: ANGLE AND PERPENDICULARITY ANALYSIS
- Angles: No right angles are explicitly marked.
- Angles: Angle NXM = Angle ZXY since they are the same angle.
- Because MN and YZ are parallel, angles XMN and XYZ are congruent.
- Because MN and YZ are parallel, angles XNM and XZY are congruent.

STEP 3: CONGRUENCE AND EQUALITY ANALYSIS
- Similar Triangles: Triangle(XNM) ~ Triangle(XYZ)
- Ratios: XM / XY = XN / XZ = MN / YZ

STEP 4: INTERSECTION AND POSITIONING ANALYSIS
- Point N lies on line XZ.
- Point M lies on line XY.

STEP 5: CONSTRAINT AND RELATIONSHIP SYNTHESIS
- XM + MY = XY => MY = XY - XM = 10 - 2 = 8
- XN + NZ = XZ
- Using similarity, XM/XY = XN/XZ. Thus, 2/10 = 6/XZ. XZ = 30
- Thus, NZ = XZ - XN = 30 - 6 = 24.

STEP 6: QUESTION-DRIVEN COMPLETENESS CHECK
- Need to find NZ
- We know XM, XY, XN
- We know that the two triangles are similar and MN || YZ.
