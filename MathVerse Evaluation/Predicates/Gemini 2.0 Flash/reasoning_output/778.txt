Question: In the figure above, triangle ABC is inscribed in the circle with center O and diameter AC. If AB = AO, what is the degree measure of angle ABO?
================================================================================

STEP 1: COMPREHENSIVE IMAGE ANALYSIS
The image shows a circle with center O and points A, B, and C on the circle. Triangle ABC is inscribed in the circle. AC is a diameter. AB = AO.

STEP 2: CIRCLE-SPECIFIC ANALYSIS
O is the center of the circle. A, B, and C are points on the circle. AC is a diameter of the circle. AO, BO, and CO are radii of the circle.

STEP 3: ANGLE AND PERPENDICULARITY ANALYSIS
Angle ABC is an inscribed angle subtended by diameter AC, so angle ABC is a right angle (90 degrees).

STEP 4: CONGRUENCE AND EQUALITY ANALYSIS
AB = AO. Since AO is a radius, and BO is a radius, AO = BO. Therefore, AB = AO = BO. Triangle ABO is an equilateral triangle.

STEP 5: INTERSECTION AND POSITIONING ANALYSIS
A, O, and C are collinear, forming the diameter AC.

STEP 6: CONSTRAINT AND RELATIONSHIP SYNTHESIS
Since triangle ABO is equilateral, all angles in triangle ABO are 60 degrees. Specifically, angle ABO = 60 degrees. Triangle ABC is a right triangle with angle ABC = 90 degrees.
