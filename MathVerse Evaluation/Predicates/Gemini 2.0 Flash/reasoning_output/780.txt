Question: In triangle ABC above, angle A is 60° and triangle ABC is equilateral. The length of AB is 3, and D is the midpoint of AC. What is the length of BC?
================================================================================

STEP 1: COMPREHENSIVE IMAGE ANALYSIS
- Geometric shapes: Triangle ABC, Triangle ABD
- Points: A, B, C, D
- Lines: AB, AC, BC, BD, AD, DC
- Measurements: AB = 3, Angle A = 60°, Angle ADB = 60°
- Special markings: Angle ADB = 60°
- D is on the line AC

STEP 2: CIRCLE-SPECIFIC ANALYSIS
- No circles present

STEP 3: ANGLE AND PERPENDICULARITY ANALYSIS
- Angle A = 60°
- Angle ADB = 60°
- Angle ABD = 180° - 60° - 60° = 60°
- Therefore, triangle ABD is equilateral

STEP 4: CONGRUENCE AND EQUALITY ANALYSIS
- Since triangle ABD is equilateral, AB = AD = BD = 3.
- Triangle ABC is equilateral so AB = BC = AC

STEP 5: INTERSECTION AND POSITIONING ANALYSIS
- Point D lies on line AC.

STEP 6: CONSTRAINT AND RELATIONSHIP SYNTHESIS
- Triangle ABD is equilateral.
- Triangle ABC is equilateral.
- D is on AC
- Therefore, AD + DC = AC

STEP 7: QUESTION-DRIVEN COMPLETENESS CHECK
- Need to represent all relationships to calculate the length of BC.
- We have AB = 3
- Since triangle ABC is equilateral AB = BC = AC = 3
- D is the midpoint of AC. This implies that AD = DC.
