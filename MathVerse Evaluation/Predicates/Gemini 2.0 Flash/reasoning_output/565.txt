Question: g(x) is shown in the figure, which is a quadratic function going through (0,-2), (-1,0), (2,0), (3,4), (-2,4). f(x)=x^2+x-6. How many roots do the functions have in common?
================================================================================

STEP 1: COMPREHENSIVE IMAGE ANALYSIS
- The image shows a graph of a quadratic function y = g(x).
- Points on the graph: (-2, 4), (-1, 0), (0, -2), (2, 0), (3, 4).
- Also given function f(x) = x^2 + x - 6.

STEP 2: FUNCTION ANALYSIS
- g(x) is a quadratic function.
- f(x) = x^2 + x - 6 = (x+3)(x-2). Roots are x=-3, x=2.
- g(x) passes through (-1, 0) and (2, 0). Roots are x=-1, x=2.
- The common root is x=2.
