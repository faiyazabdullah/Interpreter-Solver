Question: As shown in the figure, BC and BA intersect at point B, BD bisects angle ABC, CD parallel AB, if angle BCD = 70.0, then the degree of angle CDB is ()
================================================================================

STEP 1: COMPREHENSIVE IMAGE ANALYSIS
- The figure consists of lines, points and angles.
- Points: A, B, C, D
- Lines: AB, BC, CD, BD
- Angles: Angle BCD = 70 degrees.
- AB and CD are parallel
- BD bisects angle ABC.

STEP 2: ANGLE AND PERPENDICULARITY ANALYSIS
- Angle BCD is 70 degrees.
- CD is parallel to AB.
- BD bisects angle ABC, meaning that angle ABD = angle CBD.

STEP 3: CONGRUENCE AND EQUALITY ANALYSIS
- Parallel lines create equal alternate interior angles. Therefore, angle ABD = angle CDB

STEP 4: INTERSECTION AND POSITIONING ANALYSIS
- BC and BA intersect at B.
- BD intersects BC at B and CD at D.

STEP 5: CONSTRAINT AND RELATIONSHIP SYNTHESIS
- Since CD is parallel to AB, angle ABC + angle BCD = 180 degrees. Since angle BCD is 70, angle ABC must be 110.
- Since BD bisects angle ABC, angle ABD = angle CBD = 55.
- Because CD is parallel to AB, angle CDB = angle ABD. Therefore, angle CDB = 55.
