Question: As shown in the figure, AC is the diameter of circle O, if angle OBC = 40.0, then the degree of angle AOB is ()

- Measurements: Angle OBC = 40 degrees

STEP 2: CIRCLE-SPECIFIC ANALYSIS
- Center: O
- Points on circle: A, B, C
- Radii: OA, OB, OC
- Diameter: AC

STEP 3: ANGLE AND PERPENDICULARITY ANALYSIS
- Angle OBC = 40 degrees
- Need to find angle AOB
- Since OA, OB, and OC are radii, OA = OB = OC. Therefore, triangles OAB and OBC are isosceles.

STEP 4: CONGRUENCE AND EQUALITY ANALYSIS
- OA = OB = OC (radii of the same circle)
- Triangle OBC is isosceles with OB = OC, therefore Angle OCB = Angle OBC = 40 degrees.
- Triangle OAB is isosceles with OA = OB, therefore Angle OAB = Angle OBA.

STEP 5: INTERSECTION AND POSITIONING ANALYSIS
- Point O is the center of the circle
- Points A, B, and C lie on the circumference of the circle

STEP 6: CONSTRAINT AND RELATIONSHIP SYNTHESIS
- The sum of angles in Triangle OBC is 180 degrees, so Angle BOC = 180 - 40 - 40 = 100 degrees.
- Since AC is the diameter, Angle AOC is a straight angle, therefore Angle AOC = 180 degrees.
- Angle AOB + Angle BOC = Angle AOC, therefore Angle AOB + 100 = 180, Angle AOB = 80 degrees.
- This confirms the central angle AOB subtends the arc AB, and inscribed angle ACB also subtends the arc AB. Then Angle AOB = 2 * Angle ACB. Since Angle AOB = 80, Angle ACB = 40.
