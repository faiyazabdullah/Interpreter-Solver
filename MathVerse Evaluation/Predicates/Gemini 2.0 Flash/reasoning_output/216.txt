Question: There are two right triangles D F E and A C B. Find D E.
================================================================================

STEP 1: COMPREHENSIVE IMAGE ANALYSIS
- Identify all geometric shapes: Two triangles, Triangle(DFE) and Triangle(ACB).
- List all points: A, B, C, D, E, F
- List all lines: Line(DF), Line(FE), Line(DE), Line(AC), Line(CB), Line(AB)
- Note all visible measurements: LengthOf(CB) = 15, LengthOf(AC) = 45, LengthOf(FE) = 3.
- Identify all special markings: Right angle at F in Triangle(DFE), Right angle at C in Triangle(ACB). Angle at D equals Angle at A.
- Look for implied constructions: No implied constructions are apparent.

STEP 2: CIRCLE-SPECIFIC ANALYSIS
- No circles in the diagram.

STEP 3: ANGLE AND PERPENDICULARITY ANALYSIS
- Right angle at F: Angle(DFE) = 90 degrees. Perpendicular(Line(DF), Line(FE)).
- Right angle at C: Angle(ACB) = 90 degrees. Perpendicular(Line(AC), Line(CB)).
- Angle D is equal to Angle A. MeasureOf(Angle(D)) = MeasureOf(Angle(A)).

STEP 4: CONGRUENCE AND EQUALITY ANALYSIS
- Angle D is equal to Angle A. The triangles are similar because they have two equal angles.

STEP 5: INTERSECTION AND POSITIONING ANALYSIS
- Lines intersect at points to form triangles.

STEP 6: CONSTRAINT AND RELATIONSHIP SYNTHESIS
- Since Angle D = Angle A, and Angle F = Angle C = 90 degrees, then Triangle(DFE) ~ Triangle(ACB).
- The ratio of corresponding sides is equal. DF/AC = FE/CB = DE/AB

STEP 7: QUESTION-DRIVEN COMPLETENESS CHECK
- We are given AC=45, CB=15, FE=3. We want to find DE.
- Triangles are similar, so FE/CB = DE/AB. 3/15 = DE/AB => 1/5 = DE/AB. We need AB.
- Triangle ACB is a right triangle. AB^2 = AC^2 + CB^2 = 45^2 + 15^2 = 2025 + 225 = 2250.
- AB = sqrt(2250) = 15*sqrt(10).
- 1/5 = DE/(15*sqrt(10)). DE = 3*sqrt(10).
