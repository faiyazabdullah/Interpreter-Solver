Question: Lines l, m, and n are perpendicular bisectors of \triangle P Q R and meet at T. If T Q = 2 x, P T = 3 y - 1, T Q = 2 x, and T R = 8, find z.
================================================================================

STEP 1: COMPREHENSIVE IMAGE ANALYSIS
- Shapes: Triangle PQR
- Points: P, Q, R, T
- Lines: l, m, n, PT, QT, RT, PR, PQ, QR
- Measurements: TQ = 2x, PT = 3y - 1, TR = 8, distance on PR is 7, PR = z + 4
- Special Markings: Lines l, m, and n are perpendicular bisectors. Therefore T is the circumcenter of Triangle PQR.
- Since T is the circumcenter, PT = QT = RT.

STEP 2: CIRCLE-SPECIFIC ANALYSIS
- There is no explicit circle but T is the circumcenter of Triangle PQR, so a circle centered at T with radius PT = QT = RT would circumscribe Triangle PQR.

STEP 3: ANGLE AND PERPENDICULARITY ANALYSIS
- Lines l, m, and n are perpendicular bisectors of the sides of the triangle PQR, implying they are perpendicular to sides PR, QR, and PQ respectively.

STEP 4: CONGRUENCE AND EQUALITY ANALYSIS
- PT = QT = RT since T is the circumcenter.

STEP 5: INTERSECTION AND POSITIONING ANALYSIS
- Lines l, m, and n intersect at point T.
- Point T lies inside the Triangle PQR.

STEP 6: CONSTRAINT AND RELATIONSHIP SYNTHESIS
- PT = QT = RT = circumradius
- PR = z + 4, segment of PR = 7. So PR = z + 4
