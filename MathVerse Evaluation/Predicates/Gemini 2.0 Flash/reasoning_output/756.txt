Question: In quadrilateral PQRS above, the length of QR is 3 and the length of SR is 4. Angle P and angle R are right angles. The length of QP and PS are respectively a and b. What is the value of a^2 + b^2?
================================================================================

STEP 1: COMPREHENSIVE IMAGE ANALYSIS
- Geometric shapes: Quadrilateral PQRS, triangles PQS and QRS.
- Points: P, Q, R, S
- Lines: PQ (length a), QR (length 3), RS (length 2), PS (length b), QS (implied diagonal)
- Angles: Angle P (right angle), Angle R (right angle)
- Measurements: QR = 3, RS = 2, PQ = a, PS = b
- Special markings: Right angle at P and R.

STEP 2: ANGLE AND PERPENDICULARITY ANALYSIS
- Angle P is a right angle.
- Angle R is a right angle.
- PQ is perpendicular to PS.
- QR is perpendicular to RS.

STEP 3: PYTHAGOREAN THEOREM
- Triangle PQS and QRS are right triangles.
- In Triangle QRS, QS^2 = QR^2 + RS^2 = 3^2 + 2^2 = 9 + 4 = 13.
- In Triangle PQS, QS^2 = PQ^2 + PS^2 = a^2 + b^2.
- Therefore, a^2 + b^2 = QS^2 = 13.
