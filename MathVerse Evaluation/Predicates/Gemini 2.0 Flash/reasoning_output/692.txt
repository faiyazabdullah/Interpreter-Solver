Question: A 13 cm concrete cylindrical pipe has an outer radius of 6 cm and an inner radius of 4 cm as shown. Find the volume of concrete required to make the pipe, correct to two decimal places.

- The shape is a 3D object, but we can determine the volume based on the 2D measurements given.
- No specific point names or line names are provided.
- There are two concentric circles defining the inner and outer radii.

STEP 2: CIRCLE-SPECIFIC ANALYSIS

STEP 3: ANGLE AND PERPENDICULARITY ANALYSIS

STEP 4: CONGRUENCE AND EQUALITY ANALYSIS

- The pipe's volume is the volume of the outer cylinder minus the volume of the inner cylinder.
- Volume of cylinder = pi * r^2 * h, where r is radius and h is the height (or length here).
