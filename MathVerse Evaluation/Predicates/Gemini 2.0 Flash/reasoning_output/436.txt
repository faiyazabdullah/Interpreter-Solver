================================================================================
Question: There is a hyperbola in blue with double arrows intersects with y-axis at -4 and 4. Its asymptote in dashed orange is $y=(4/5)x$ and $y=-(4/5)x$. There is also a green rectangular tangent to the hyperbola. Find the equation of the hyperbola.
================================================================================

STEP 1: COMPREHENSIVE IMAGE ANALYSIS
- Geometric shapes: Hyperbola, rectangle, two intersecting lines (asymptotes), coordinate axes
- Points: Origin (0,0), intersections of hyperbola with y-axis, corners of the rectangle.
- Lines: x-axis, y-axis, asymptotes, sides of the rectangle, hyperbola.
- Measurements: Hyperbola intersects y-axis at y = 4 and y = -4. Asymptotes have slopes 4/5 and -4/5.
- Special markings: Dashed lines for asymptotes, dashed lines for the rectangle, double arrow curves for the hyperbola.
- Implied constructions: The asymptotes pass through the origin. The rectangle is centered at the origin.

STEP 2: CIRCLE-SPECIFIC ANALYSIS
- There are no circles.

STEP 3: ANGLE AND PERPENDICULARITY ANALYSIS
- x and y axes are perpendicular.
- Asymptotes are symmetric with respect to the x-axis and y-axis.

STEP 4: CONGRUENCE AND EQUALITY ANALYSIS
- The rectangle appears symmetric with respect to both x and y axes.

STEP 5: INTERSECTION AND POSITIONING ANALYSIS
- The asymptotes intersect at the origin.
- Hyperbola intersects the y-axis at (0,4) and (0,-4).
- Rectangle is tangent to the hyperbola.

STEP 6: CONSTRAINT AND RELATIONSHIP SYNTHESIS
- The hyperbola is centered at the origin.
- The asymptotes are y = (4/5)x and y = -(4/5)x.

STEP 7: QUESTION-DRIVEN COMPLETENESS CHECK
- Equation of the hyperbola: $\frac{y^2}{a^2} - \frac{x^2}{b^2} = 1$
- a = 4 (since the hyperbola intersects y-axis at 4 and -4)
- The slopes of the asymptotes are $\pm \frac{a}{b} = \pm \frac{4}{b}$
- Given that the asymptotes are y = (4/5)x and y = -(4/5)x, we have $\frac{4}{b} = \frac{4}{5}$, therefore b = 5.
- So, the equation is $\frac{y^2}{16} - \frac{x^2}{25} = 1$.
