- Shapes: Circle, triangle (implied)
- Points: A, B, C, D, O
- Lines: AB, OC, OD, AD, BC
- Angles: Angle D = 34 degrees
- Special Markings: O is the center of the circle, AB is the diameter.

STEP 2: CIRCLE-SPECIFIC ANALYSIS
- Center: O
- Points on circle: A, B, C, D
- Radius: OA, OB, OC, OD
- Diameter: AB
- Inscribed angle: Angle D
- Central angle: Angle AOC, Angle BOC

STEP 3: ANGLE AND PERPENDICULARITY ANALYSIS
- Angle D is an inscribed angle.
- Implied: Angle AOD is a central angle subtending the same arc as Angle D

STEP 4: CONGRUENCE AND EQUALITY ANALYSIS
- OA = OB = OC = OD (radii of the same circle)
- Triangle AOD is isosceles.
- Triangle BOC is isosceles.

STEP 5: INTERSECTION AND POSITIONING ANALYSIS
- AB intersects the circle at A and B.
- OC and OD intersect the circle at C and D.

STEP 6: CONSTRAINT AND RELATIONSHIP SYNTHESIS
- The measure of the central angle is twice the measure of the inscribed angle subtending the same arc. Therefore, Angle AOC = 2 * Angle D
- Angle AOC and Angle BOC are supplementary angles, so their sum is 180 degrees.
- The sum of the angles in triangle AOD is 180 degrees.
- The sum of the angles in triangle BOC is 180 degrees.

- We need to determine the measure of Angle BOC. We have the measure of Angle D. The relationship between Angle D and Angle AOC is important. The supplementary relationship between Angle AOC and Angle BOC is important.
