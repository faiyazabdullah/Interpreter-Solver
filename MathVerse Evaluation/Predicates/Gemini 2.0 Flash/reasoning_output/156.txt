Question: As shown in the figure, the quadrilateral ABCD and A′B′C′D′ are similar figures with the similar center at point O. If OA′: A′A = 2.0:1.0, the area of ​​the quadrilateral A′B′C′D′ is 12.0 ^ 2, then the area of ​​the quadrilateral ABCD is ()
================================================================================

STEP 1: COMPREHENSIVE IMAGE ANALYSIS
- Shapes: Two quadrilaterals ABCD and A'B'C'D', which are similar.
- Points: A, B, C, D, A', B', C', D', O
- Lines: Lines connecting the points.
- Measurements: OA':A'A = 2:1, Area(A'B'C'D') = 12
- Similar figures A'B'C'D' and ABCD with center O.

STEP 2: CIRCLE-SPECIFIC ANALYSIS
- No circles present.

STEP 3: ANGLE AND PERPENDICULARITY ANALYSIS
- No explicit right angles or perpendicular relationships.

STEP 4: CONGRUENCE AND EQUALITY ANALYSIS
- Similar(Quadrilateral(ABCD),Quadrilateral(A'B'C'D'))
- OA'/A'A = 2/1, therefore OA' = 2*A'A.
Since OA = OA' + A'A, then OA = 2*A'A + A'A = 3*A'A
Therefore, OA'/OA = (2*A'A) / (3*A'A) = 2/3. This is the similarity ratio.

STEP 5: INTERSECTION AND POSITIONING ANALYSIS
- Point O is the center of similarity.

STEP 6: CONSTRAINT AND RELATIONSHIP SYNTHESIS
- Ratio of areas of similar figures is the square of the ratio of corresponding sides.
