Question: In rhombus L M P Q, diagonal P L and Q M intersect at R. m \angle Q L M = 2 x^ { 2 } - 10, m \angle Q P M = 8 x, and M P = 10. Find the perimeter of L M P Q.
================================================================================

STEP 1: COMPREHENSIVE IMAGE ANALYSIS
- Geometric shapes: Rhombus L M P Q, diagonals P L and Q M intersecting at R, triangles Q L M, Q P M, P M L, P Q L
- Points: L, M, P, Q, R
- Lines: L M, M P, P Q, Q L, P L, Q M
- Angles: Angle(Q L M) = 2x^2 - 10, Angle(Q P M) = 8x
- Measurements: M P = 10
- Special markings: Rhombus implies L M = M P = P Q = Q L

STEP 2: ANGLE AND PERPENDICULARITY ANALYSIS
- Diagonals of a rhombus bisect each other at right angles. Therefore Angle(L R M) = 90 degrees.
- Diagonals of a rhombus bisect the angles. Therefore Angle(Q P R) = Angle(R P M) = 4x and Angle(Q L R) = Angle(R L M) = x^2 - 5.

STEP 3: CONGRUENCE AND EQUALITY ANALYSIS
- L M P Q is a rhombus, so L M = M P = P Q = Q L
- Triangles Q L M and Q P M are congruent.
- Triangles L M R, M P R, P Q R, Q L R are congruent right triangles.

STEP 4: INTERSECTION AND POSITIONING ANALYSIS
- P L and Q M intersect at R, which is the midpoint of both diagonals.

STEP 5: CONSTRAINT AND RELATIONSHIP SYNTHESIS
- Since L M P Q is a rhombus, opposite angles are equal: Angle(Q L M) = Angle(Q P M).
- Therefore, 2x^2 - 10 = 8x
- 2x^2 - 8x - 10 = 0
- x^2 - 4x - 5 = 0
- (x-5)(x+1) = 0
- x = 5 or x = -1.
Since angles cannot be negative, x = 5.
Angle(Q L M) = 2 * 5^2 - 10 = 50 - 10 = 40 degrees.
Angle(Q P M) = 8 * 5 = 40 degrees.

STEP 6: QUESTION-DRIVEN COMPLETENESS CHECK
- We have found x = 5. L M = M P = P Q = Q L = 10.
Perimeter of L M P Q = 4 * 10 = 40.
