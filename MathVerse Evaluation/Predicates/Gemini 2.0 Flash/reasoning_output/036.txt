Question: As shown in the figure, AB is the diameter of circle O, point C is on circle O, passing point C to draw the tangent of circle O and it intersects the extended line of AB at point D. Connect AC. If angle D = 50.0, then the degree of angle A is ()
================================================================================

STEP 1: COMPREHENSIVE IMAGE ANALYSIS
- Geometric shapes: Circle, triangle
- Points: A, B, C, D, O
- Lines: AB, CD, AC, OC
- Angles: Angle D = 50 degrees, Angle A, Angle ACO, Angle OCB
- Special markings: O is the center of the circle. CD is tangent to the circle at C.
- Implied constructions: OC is radius, OC is perpendicular to CD.

STEP 2: CIRCLE-SPECIFIC ANALYSIS
- Center: O
- Points on circle: A, B, C
- Lines: AB is diameter, OC is radius, AC is chord, CD is tangent.
- Angle: Angle ABC is an inscribed angle.
- Radius relationships: OA = OB = OC

STEP 3: ANGLE AND PERPENDICULARITY ANALYSIS
- Angle D = 50 degrees
- OC is perpendicular to CD, so Angle OCD = 90 degrees
- Angle AOC is a central angle.
- Angle OCA = Angle OAC because OA = OC.

STEP 4: CONGRUENCE AND EQUALITY ANALYSIS
- OA = OB = OC (radii of the same circle)
- Triangle AOC is isosceles.

STEP 5: INTERSECTION AND POSITIONING ANALYSIS
- CD is tangent to the circle at C.
- CD intersects the extended line of AB at D.
- A, O, B lie on the line AB.

STEP 6: CONSTRAINT AND RELATIONSHIP SYNTHESIS
- Since CD is tangent to the circle O at C, OC is perpendicular to CD.
- Therefore, angle OCD = 90 degrees.
- Since angle D = 50 degrees, angle COD = 180 - 90 - 50 = 40 degrees.
- In triangle AOC, OA = OC, so angle OAC = angle OCA.
- Angle AOC = 180 - angle COD = 180 - 40 = 140 degrees
- Therefore, angle OAC = (180-140)/2 = 20 degrees.
- Therefore, angle A = 20 degrees.

STEP 7: QUESTION-DRIVEN COMPLETENESS CHECK
- All necessary information is captured to determine the measure of angle A.
