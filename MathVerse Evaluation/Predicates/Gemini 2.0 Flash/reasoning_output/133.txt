Question: As shown in the figure, in order to estimate the width of the Jing River, a target point P is selected on the opposite bank of the Jing River, and points Q and S are taken near the bank, so that the points P, Q, and S are in a straight line, and the straight line PS is perpendicular to the river. Choose an appropriate point T on the straight line a passing point S and perpendicular to PS. The intersection of PT and the straight line b passing point Q and perpendicular to PS is R. If QS = 60.0, ST = 120.0, QR = 80.0, then the width of the river PQ is ()
================================================================================

- The figure shows a geometric diagram with points P, Q, R, S, and T.
- There are line segments PQ, QR, PS, ST, PT, and QS.
- Line a passes through S and T, and line b passes through Q and R.
- PS is perpendicular to line a (ST) and line b (QR).
- QS = 60, ST = 120, and QR = 80.
- Triangles PQS, PRQ and PST are present. Also triangle PRT.

STEP 2: CIRCLE-SPECIFIC ANALYSIS
- There are no circles in this problem.

STEP 3: ANGLE AND PERPENDICULARITY ANALYSIS
- Angle PSQ is a right angle.
- Angle QRS is a right angle.
- Angle PST is a right angle.
- Line PS is perpendicular to line a (containing S and T).
- Line PQ is a transversal across parallel lines a and b.
- Line PS is perpendicular to line b (containing Q and R).
- Angle QRT and Angle PSR are right angles.

STEP 4: CONGRUENCE AND EQUALITY ANALYSIS
- Triangles PQR and PTS are similar.

STEP 5: INTERSECTION AND POSITIONING ANALYSIS
- Point Q lies on line PS.
- Point S lies on line PS.
- Point R lies on line PT.
- Point T lies on line a.
- Point Q lies on line b.

STEP 6: CONSTRAINT AND RELATIONSHIP SYNTHESIS
- Triangles PQR and PTS are similar by AA similarity since they both have a right angle and share the angle at P.
- Using similar triangle properties, we have PQ/PS = QR/ST. Also, PR/PT=QR/ST
- PS = PQ + QS
- So, PQ/(PQ+QS) = QR/ST or PQ/(PQ+60) = 80/120
- So, PQ/(PQ+60) = 2/3
- 3PQ = 2PQ + 120
- PQ = 120
