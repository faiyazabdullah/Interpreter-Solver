Question: As shown in the figure, the student Xiao Li whose height is 1.6 wants to measure the height of the school's flagpole. When he stands at C, the shadow of the top of his head coincides with the shadow of the top of the flagpole, and AC = 2.0, BC = 8.0, then the height of the flagpole is ()
================================================================================

STEP 1: COMPREHENSIVE IMAGE ANALYSIS
The image shows a flagpole and a person (Xiao Li) standing on the ground. The person's height is given as 1.6. The distance from the person to point A is 2.0, and the distance from A to B is 8. The person is standing at point C. The top of the person's head aligns with the top of the flagpole with respect to the observer at point A. There is an implied perpendicular relationship between the flagpole and the ground, and also between the person and the ground. Triangles are formed.

STEP 2: ANGLE AND PERPENDICULARITY ANALYSIS
Angle CAB is common to both triangles. The person and the flagpole are assumed to be perpendicular to the ground. So we have similar triangles.

STEP 3: CONGRUENCE AND EQUALITY ANALYSIS
The triangles formed are similar due to AA similarity (Angle-Angle).

STEP 4: INTERSECTION AND POSITIONING ANALYSIS
Points A, C, and B are collinear.

STEP 5: CONSTRAINT AND RELATIONSHIP SYNTHESIS
The height of the person is 1.6. AC = 2 and CB = 8. Thus, AB = AC + CB = 2 + 8 = 10. We have two similar triangles. Let the height of the flagpole be h.  The ratio of corresponding sides of the similar triangles are equal. So, 1.6/h = 2/10
