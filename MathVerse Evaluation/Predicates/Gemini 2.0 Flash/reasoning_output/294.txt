Question: In parallelogram J K L M, diagonals J L and K M intersect at point R, and ML is 45, KL is 21. Find m \angle K J L if J K = 2 b + 3 and J M = 3 a.
================================================================================

- Geometric Shapes: Parallelogram, triangles.
- Points: J, K, L, M, R.
- Lines: JK, KL, LM, MJ, JL, KM.
- Measurements: ML = 45, KL = 21, JK = 2b+3, JM = 3a, Angle(M,L,K) = 30 degrees, Angle(K,L,J) = 70 degrees.
- Special Markings: Parallelogram JKLM

STEP 2: ANGLE AND PERPENDICULARITY ANALYSIS
- Angle MLK = 30 degrees, Angle KLJ = 70 degrees.
- Angle MJK is not explicitly given.
- Diagonals JL and KM intersect at point R.

STEP 3: CONGRUENCE AND EQUALITY ANALYSIS
- Opposite sides of a parallelogram are equal: JK = ML, JM = KL.
- Opposite angles of a parallelogram are equal: Angle(JML) = Angle(JKL), Angle(MJK) = Angle(KLM).
- Diagonals of a parallelogram bisect each other: JR = RL, KR = RM.

STEP 4: INTERSECTION AND POSITIONING ANALYSIS
- Lines JL and KM intersect at point R.

STEP 5: CONSTRAINT AND RELATIONSHIP SYNTHESIS
- ML = JK = 45, so 2b+3 = 45.
- JM = KL = 21, so 3a = 21.
- Angle KLM = Angle MLK + Angle KLJ = 30+70 = 100 degrees
- Angle JML = supplementary to Angle KLM, so Angle JML = 180 - 100 = 80 degrees
- Angle JKL = Angle JML = 80 degrees
- Angle MJK = supplementary to Angle JML = 180-80 = 80 degrees
