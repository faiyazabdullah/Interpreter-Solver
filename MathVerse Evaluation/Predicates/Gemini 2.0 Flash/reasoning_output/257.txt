================================================================================
Question: Two triangles A B E and A C D are similar with a shared vertex A. B E and C D are parallel. If A B = 12, A C = 16, and E D = 5, find A E.
================================================================================

STEP 1: COMPREHENSIVE IMAGE ANALYSIS
- Identify geometric shapes: Triangles, lines, points.
- Points: A, B, C, D, E
- Lines: AB, AC, AD, AE, BE, CD
- Measurements: AB = 12, AC = 16, ED = 5
- Parallel lines: BE || CD
- Similar triangles: ABE ~ ACD

STEP 2: ANGLE AND PERPENDICULARITY ANALYSIS
- No right angles are explicitly marked. However, due to parallel lines, some angles are equal.
- Angle BAE = Angle CAD (shared angle)
- Angle ABE = Angle ACD (corresponding angles, BE || CD)
- Angle AEB = Angle ADC (corresponding angles, BE || CD)

STEP 3: CONGRUENCE AND EQUALITY ANALYSIS
- Triangles ABE and ACD are similar.
- No congruent shapes are explicitly marked.

STEP 4: INTERSECTION AND POSITIONING ANALYSIS
- Lines AB and AE intersect at A
- Lines AC and AD intersect at A
- Lines BE and CD are parallel

STEP 5: CONSTRAINT AND RELATIONSHIP SYNTHESIS
- The similarity of triangles ABE and ACD implies proportional side lengths: AB/AC = AE/AD = BE/CD

STEP 6: QUESTION-DRIVEN COMPLETENESS CHECK
- We have all the information necessary to establish the proportion and solve for AE.
