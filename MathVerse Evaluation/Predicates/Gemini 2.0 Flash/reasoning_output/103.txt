Question: As shown in the figure, the points A, B, and C are on circle O, and it is known that angle ABC = 130.0, then angle AOC = ()
================================================================================

STEP 1: COMPREHENSIVE IMAGE ANALYSIS
- Circle: Circle O
- Points on the circle: A, B, C
- Angle: Angle ABC = 130 degrees
- Radii: OA, OC
- Chords: AB, BC, AC
- Central angle: Angle AOC

STEP 2: CIRCLE-SPECIFIC ANALYSIS
- Point O is the center of the circle.
- Points A, B, and C lie on circle O.
- OA and OC are radii of circle O.
- Angle ABC is an inscribed angle.
- Angle AOC is a central angle.
- The inscribed angle ABC intercepts arc AC.
- The central angle AOC also intercepts arc AC.
- The measure of central angle AOC is twice the measure of inscribed angle ABC only if angle ABC is acute.
-Since angle ABC = 130, the reflex angle AOC = 2*130 = 260.
-Then the smaller angle AOC would be 360 - 260 = 100.

STEP 3: ANGLE AND PERPENDICULARITY ANALYSIS
- Angle ABC = 130 degrees is given.
- No right angles or perpendicular relationships are explicitly marked.

STEP 4: CONGRUENCE AND EQUALITY ANALYSIS
- OA = OC because they are radii of the same circle.

STEP 5: INTERSECTION AND POSITIONING ANALYSIS
- Points A, B, and C lie on the circle.

STEP 6: CONSTRAINT AND RELATIONSHIP SYNTHESIS
- Angle ABC is an inscribed angle intercepting arc AC.
- Angle AOC is a central angle intercepting arc AC.
- m(angle AOC) = 2 * m(angle ABC) if ABC < 90.
-However, since angle ABC = 130 > 90, then the central angle = 360 - 2 * angle ABC's supplement.
The supplement of 130 is 50. Therefore angle AOC = 2 * 50 = 100.
