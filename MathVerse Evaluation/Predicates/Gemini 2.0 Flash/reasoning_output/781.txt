Question: In the figure above, the figure consists of a quadrilateral and a triangle. CDE is an equilateral triangle and ABCE is a square with an area of 1. What is the perimeter of polygon ABCDE?
================================================================================

STEP 1: COMPREHENSIVE IMAGE ANALYSIS
- Geometric shapes: Square, Equilateral Triangle, Pentagon
- Points: A, B, C, D, E
- Lines: AB, BC, CE, AE, CD, DE
- Measurements: Area of square ABCE = 1
- Special Markings: The sides of the square ABCE are equal in length. The sides of the equilateral triangle CDE are equal in length. Angles in the square are right angles. Angles in equilateral triangle are 60 degrees.

STEP 2: ANGLE AND PERPENDICULARITY ANALYSIS
- Angles in square ABCE are right angles, therefore AB is perpendicular to AE, BC is perpendicular to AB, etc.
- Triangle CDE is equilateral, so angles DCE, CED, and EDC are equal to 60 degrees.

STEP 3: CONGRUENCE AND EQUALITY ANALYSIS
- Since ABCE is a square, AB=BC=CE=AE.
- Since CDE is an equilateral triangle, CD=DE=CE. Therefore AB=BC=CE=AE=CD=DE.

STEP 4: INTERSECTION AND POSITIONING ANALYSIS
- Sides of square and triangle intersect at points C and E.

STEP 5: CONSTRAINT AND RELATIONSHIP SYNTHESIS
- The square ABCE and the equilateral triangle CDE share a common side CE.
- Knowing the area of the square allows us to determine the length of its sides, which are also the sides of the equilateral triangle.

STEP 6: QUESTION-DRIVEN COMPLETENESS CHECK
- We need to represent the shapes, their properties, and equality relationships. We also need to represent the area of the square. This should be sufficient to determine the perimeter of the pentagon ABCDE.
