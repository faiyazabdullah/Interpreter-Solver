Question: There is a function representing a circle centered at the origin with a radius of 1 unit. Points A,B, and C are plotted on the unit circle. Which of the coordinates is equal to \sin \left(310^{\circ}\right) ?
================================================================================

STEP 1: COMPREHENSIVE IMAGE ANALYSIS
- Shapes: Circle with points A, B, and C on the circumference. x and y axes.
- Points: A, B, C, origin (0,0)
- Lines: Circle, x-axis, y-axis
- Measurements: The circle seems to be a unit circle.
- Special markings: None explicitly marked other than axes and coordinate grid.

STEP 2: CIRCLE-SPECIFIC ANALYSIS
- Center: Origin (0,0)
- Points on circle: A, B, C
- Radius: 1 (implied by the grid)

STEP 3: ANGLE AND PERPENDICULARITY ANALYSIS
- x and y axes are perpendicular.

STEP 4: CONGRUENCE AND EQUALITY ANALYSIS
- All radii are equal to 1.

STEP 5: INTERSECTION AND POSITIONING ANALYSIS
- The x and y axes intersect at the origin (0,0) which is also the center of the circle.

STEP 6: CONSTRAINT AND RELATIONSHIP SYNTHESIS
- The circle is centered at the origin and has a radius of 1. This makes it the unit circle.

- The location of points A, B, and C on the circle are important. The problem relates to \sin \left(310^{\circ}\right), so the y-coordinates of A, B, and C relative to the angle are required.
