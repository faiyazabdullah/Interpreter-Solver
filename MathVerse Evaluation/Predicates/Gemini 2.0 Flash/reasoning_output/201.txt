- Measurements: Angle MTN = 28 degrees, the distance from M to AC is equal to the distance from M to TN
- Special markings: Right angle symbols where perpendicular lines intersect AC and TN, congruent marks on the perpendicular distances from M to AC and TN.
- Implied constructions: radii MC, MA, MT, MN.

STEP 2: CIRCLE-SPECIFIC ANALYSIS

- Points on the circle: C, A, T, N
- Radii: MC, MA, MT, MN
- Chords: AC, TN
- Equal radii relationships: MC = MA = MT = MN

STEP 3: ANGLE AND PERPENDICULARITY ANALYSIS
- Angle MTN = 28 degrees
- Right angles: M is perpendicular to AC, M is perpendicular to TN

STEP 4: CONGRUENCE AND EQUALITY ANALYSIS
- Equal lengths: The perpendicular distance from M to AC is equal to the perpendicular distance from M to TN
- Equal radii: MC = MA = MT = MN

STEP 5: INTERSECTION AND POSITIONING ANALYSIS
- Intersection: The perpendicular from M intersects AC and TN

STEP 6: CONSTRAINT AND RELATIONSHIP SYNTHESIS
- Since the distances from M to the chords AC and TN are equal, the chords AC and TN are equidistant from the center.
- A chord's perpendicular bisector passes through the center of the circle
