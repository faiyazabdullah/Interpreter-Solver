Question: The radius of the figure is $ 80.2 \mathrm{~mm}$. Consider the sector below.

Calculate the perimeter. Round your answer to two decimal places.
================================================================================

STEP 1: COMPREHENSIVE IMAGE ANALYSIS
- The image shows a sector of a circle.
- The radius of the circle is given as 80.2 mm.
- The central angle of the sector is given as 237 degrees.
- We need to calculate the perimeter of the sector.

STEP 2: CIRCLE-SPECIFIC ANALYSIS
- Let the center of the circle be O.
- Let the two endpoints of the arc be A and B.
- OA and OB are radii of the circle.
- LengthOf(OA) = LengthOf(OB) = 80.2 mm.
- The angle AOB is 237 degrees.

STEP 3: ANGLE AND PERPENDICULARITY ANALYSIS
- Angle AOB is given as 237 degrees.
- There are no explicit right angle indicators.

STEP 4: CONGRUENCE AND EQUALITY ANALYSIS
- OA = OB (both are radii of the same circle)

STEP 5: INTERSECTION AND POSITIONING ANALYSIS
- The lines OA and OB meet at point O.
- Points A and B lie on the circle.

STEP 6: CONSTRAINT AND RELATIONSHIP SYNTHESIS
- The perimeter of the sector is the sum of the two radii and the arc length.
- Arc length = (central angle / 360) * 2 * pi * radius
- Perimeter = 2 * radius + (central angle / 360) * 2 * pi * radius
- Perimeter = 2 * 80.2 + (237 / 360) * 2 * pi * 80.2
- Perimeter = 160.4 + (0.6583) * 2 * 3.14159 * 80.2
- Perimeter = 160.4 + 331.79
- Perimeter = 492.19
