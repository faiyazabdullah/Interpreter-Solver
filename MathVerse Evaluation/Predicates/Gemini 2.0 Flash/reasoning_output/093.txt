Question: As shown in the figure, points A, B, and C are all on circle O, when angle OBC = 40.0, the degree of angle A is ()
================================================================================

STEP 1: COMPREHENSIVE IMAGE ANALYSIS
- We have a circle with center O.
- Points A, B, and C lie on the circle.
- Line segments OA, OB, and OC are radii of the circle.
- Angle OBC is given as 40 degrees.
- Angle BAC is an inscribed angle subtending arc BC.
- Angle BOC is a central angle subtending arc BC.

STEP 2: CIRCLE-SPECIFIC ANALYSIS
- Circle with center O.
- Points A, B, and C are on the circle.
- OA, OB, and OC are radii.  Therefore, OA = OB = OC.
- Angle BAC is an inscribed angle subtending arc BC.
- Angle BOC is a central angle subtending arc BC.
- Angle BOC = 2 * Angle BAC (inscribed angle theorem).

STEP 3: ANGLE AND PERPENDICULARITY ANALYSIS
- Angle OBC = 40 degrees.
- Triangle OBC is isosceles since OB = OC.
- Therefore, Angle OCB = Angle OBC = 40 degrees.
- Angle BOC = 180 - Angle OBC - Angle OCB = 180 - 40 - 40 = 100 degrees.
- Angle BAC = 1/2 * Angle BOC = 1/2 * 100 = 50 degrees.

STEP 4: CONGRUENCE AND EQUALITY ANALYSIS
- OB = OC (radii)
- Angle OBC = Angle OCB

STEP 5: INTERSECTION AND POSITIONING ANALYSIS
- Lines intersect at points A, B, C, and O.
- Points A, B, and C lie on the circle.

STEP 6: CONSTRAINT AND RELATIONSHIP SYNTHESIS
- Triangle OBC is isosceles because OB=OC.
- Angle BOC is the central angle that corresponds to the inscribed angle BAC.
- Angle BAC = 1/2 * Angle BOC.

STEP 7: QUESTION-DRIVEN COMPLETENESS CHECK
- We need to find angle A (angle BAC).
- We know Angle OBC = 40.
- We can deduce that Angle BOC = 100.
- Then we can use Angle BAC = 1/2 * Angle BOC to find angle BAC.
