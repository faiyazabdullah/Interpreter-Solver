- Lines: AB, AC, BC, CD, OD.
- Angles: Angle DOB = 140 degrees.
- AB is a diameter. O is the center of the circle.

STEP 2: CIRCLE-SPECIFIC ANALYSIS
- Center: O. Points on circle: A, B, C, D.
- Radii: OA, OB, OC, OD.
- Diameter: AB.
- Chords: AC, BC, CD.
- Inscribed angles: Angle ACB, Angle ACD.
- Central Angle: Angle DOB.
- OA = OB = OC = OD (equal radii).

STEP 3: ANGLE AND PERPENDICULARITY ANALYSIS
- Angle DOB = 140 degrees.
- Since AB is a diameter, angle ACB = 90 degrees (inscribed angle subtending diameter).
- Angle AOD and Angle DOB are supplementary.
- Angle AOD = 180 - 140 = 40 degrees.
- Angle ACD is an inscribed angle subtending arc AD.
- Angle AOD is a central angle subtending arc AD.
- Measure of arc AD = measure of Angle AOD = 40 degrees.
- Measure of Angle ACD = 1/2 * measure of arc AD = 1/2 * 40 = 20 degrees.

STEP 4: CONGRUENCE AND EQUALITY ANALYSIS
- OA = OB = OC = OD.
- Triangle OAD is isosceles (OA = OD).
- Triangle OBC is isosceles (OB = OC).

STEP 5: INTERSECTION AND POSITIONING ANALYSIS
- Lines intersect at various points.
- Points lie on the circle.

STEP 6: CONSTRAINT AND RELATIONSHIP SYNTHESIS
- Angle ACB = 90 degrees because it subtends a diameter.
- Inscribed angle is half of the central angle subtending the same arc.
