Question: As shown in the figure, point P is a point on the extended line AB of the diameter of circle O, passing point P to draw the tangent PC of circle O, and the tangent point is C. If AO = OB = PB = 1.0, then the length of PC is ()
================================================================================

STEP 1: COMPREHENSIVE IMAGE ANALYSIS
- Geometric shapes: Circle, line segments.
- Points: A, O, B, P, C.
- Lines: AB, PC.
- Measurements: AO = 1, OB = 1, PB = 1.
- Special markings: Tangent at point C.

STEP 2: CIRCLE-SPECIFIC ANALYSIS
- Center: O
- Points on circle: A, B, C.
- Radius: OA, OB, OC.
- Diameter: AB.
- Tangent: PC.
- OA = OB = OC = 1 (equal radii)

STEP 3: ANGLE AND PERPENDICULARITY ANALYSIS
- Angle OCP is a right angle because PC is tangent to the circle at C.
- Therefore, Line OC is perpendicular to Line PC.

STEP 4: CONGRUENCE AND EQUALITY ANALYSIS
- AO = OB = PB = 1.
- OA = OB = OC (radii)

STEP 5: INTERSECTION AND POSITIONING ANALYSIS
- P lies on the extension of line AB.
- C lies on the circle.
- PC is tangent to the circle at C.

STEP 6: CONSTRAINT AND RELATIONSHIP SYNTHESIS
- AB is the diameter, and P lies on the extension of the diameter.
- PC is tangent at C, therefore OC is perpendicular to PC.
- OP = OB + BP = 1 + 1 = 2.
- OCP is a right triangle.
