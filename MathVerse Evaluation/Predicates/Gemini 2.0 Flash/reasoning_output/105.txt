Question: As shown in the figure, AB is the diameter of circle O, CD is the chord of circle O, and the extended lines of AB and CD intersect at point E. Given that AB = 2 DE, angle E = 16.0, then the degree of angle ABC is ()
================================================================================

STEP 1: COMPREHENSIVE IMAGE ANALYSIS
- We have a circle, lines, and angles.
- Points: A, B, C, D, E, O are labeled.
- Line AB is a diameter. Line CD is a chord. Lines AB and CD intersect at E.
- Angle E = 16 degrees. AB = 2DE.

STEP 2: CIRCLE-SPECIFIC ANALYSIS
- Circle with center O. Points A, B, C are on the circle.
- AB is the diameter. OB is a radius, OA is a radius.
- CD is a chord.

STEP 3: ANGLE AND PERPENDICULARITY ANALYSIS
- Angle E is given as 16 degrees.
- We can construct OC to form triangle OCE.

STEP 4: CONGRUENCE AND EQUALITY ANALYSIS
- OA = OB = OC since all are radii.

STEP 5: INTERSECTION AND POSITIONING ANALYSIS
- Lines AB and CD intersect at E.
- O is the midpoint of AB.

STEP 6: CONSTRAINT AND RELATIONSHIP SYNTHESIS
- We are given that AB = 2DE. Since OB = OA = radius, then 2 * radius = 2DE, implying radius = DE. Therefore OC = DE.
- In triangle OCE, OC = DE, and angle E = 16.
