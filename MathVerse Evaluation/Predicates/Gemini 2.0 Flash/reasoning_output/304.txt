Question: \triangle R S V \cong \triangle T V S, and they share a side S V. S V is perpendicular to S R and \angle R V S = 78 degrees. Find x.

- List points: R, S, V, T
- Visible measurements: RS = 2y - 1, ST = 24.5, VT = 24, Angle RVS = 78 degrees, Angle at S = 90 degrees, Angle T = x.
- Special markings: Right angle symbol at S, arcs indicating angles.
- Implied constructions: Perpendicular SV to RS.

STEP 2: CIRCLE-SPECIFIC ANALYSIS

STEP 3: ANGLE AND PERPENDICULARITY ANALYSIS
- Angle RVS = 78 degrees
- Angle RSV = 90 degrees
- SV is perpendicular to RS
- Angle TVS + Angle RVS = Angle RVT. Angle RVT appears to be a straight line.
- Angle VST + Angle TVS = 90 degrees because triangle TVS has angles that sum to 180 degrees. Angle VST is unknown.

STEP 4: CONGRUENCE AND EQUALITY ANALYSIS
- \triangle R S V \cong \triangle T V S (Given).
- SV is a common side to both triangles. LengthOf(S,V) = LengthOf(S,V)
- Due to congruence, RS = VT and RV = ST. So 2y - 1 = 24 and RV = 24.5.
- Due to congruence, Angle RSV = Angle TVS = 90 degrees and Angle VRS = Angle VTS = x.
- Sum of angles in Triangle RVS: 90 + 78 + Angle VRS = 180, Angle VRS = 180 - 90 - 78 = 12 degrees.
- Therefore Angle VTS = x = 12 degrees.

- Sum of angles in a triangle is 180 degrees.
- Definition of perpendicularity.
