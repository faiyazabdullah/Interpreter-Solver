Question: Triangle L M N is equilateral, and M P bisects L N. L M is 3 x + 1 and M N is 4 x - 2. Find the measure of the side of \triangle L M N.
================================================================================

STEP 1: COMPREHENSIVE IMAGE ANALYSIS
- Geometric shapes: Triangle LMN, Line MP
- Points: L, M, N, P
- Lines: LM, MN, LN, MP
- Measurements: LM = 3x + 1, MN = 4x - 2, Angle LMP = 5y degrees
- Special markings: Triangle LMN is equilateral, MP bisects LN.
- Implied constructions: LP = PN since MP bisects LN

STEP 2: CIRCLE-SPECIFIC ANALYSIS
- No circles are present in the diagram.

STEP 3: ANGLE AND PERPENDICULARITY ANALYSIS
- Angle LMP = 5y
- Since triangle LMN is equilateral, Angle MLN = Angle LMN = Angle MNL = 60 degrees.
- Since MP bisects LN, LP = PN, and MP is an altitude as well as a median. Thus, MP is perpendicular to LN, and Angle LPM = 90 degrees.

STEP 4: CONGRUENCE AND EQUALITY ANALYSIS
- LM = MN = LN since triangle LMN is equilateral
- LP = PN since MP bisects LN

STEP 5: INTERSECTION AND POSITIONING ANALYSIS
- MP intersects LN at point P
- Point P lies on line LN

STEP 6: CONSTRAINT AND RELATIONSHIP SYNTHESIS
- Equilateral triangle property implies all sides are equal.
- Angle bisector, median and altitude coincide in an equilateral triangle.
- Bisecting the base of an equilateral triangle creates two right triangles

STEP 7: QUESTION-DRIVEN COMPLETENESS CHECK
- All necessary geometric relationships are identified
