Question: There is a blue rectangular with four vertexes (-6,5), (-6,-1), (10,5), (10,-1). The two diagonals are red and intersect with each other at (2,2). Find the coordinates of the midpoint for each diagonal.
================================================================================

- The image shows a rectangle with vertices at (-6,5), (-6,-1), (10,5), and (10,-1).
- There are two red dashed lines representing the diagonals of the rectangle.
- The diagonals intersect at the point (2,2).
- The coordinate axes are shown as x and y.

STEP 2: DIAGONAL ANALYSIS
- Diagonal 1 connects (-6, 5) and (10, -1)
- Diagonal 2 connects (-6, -1) and (10, 5)

STEP 3: MIDPOINT ANALYSIS
- Let M1 be the midpoint of the diagonal connecting (-6, 5) and (10, -1)
- M1 = ((-6+10)/2, (5-1)/2) = (4/2, 4/2) = (2, 2)
- Let M2 be the midpoint of the diagonal connecting (-6, -1) and (10, 5)
- M2 = ((-6+10)/2, (-1+5)/2) = (4/2, 4/2) = (2, 2)
- Both midpoints are at (2,2), which is the intersection point of the diagonals.
