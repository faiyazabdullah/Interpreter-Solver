Question: As shown in the figure, circle O is the circumscribed circle of triangle ABC. Connect OB and OC, if the radius of circle O is 2.0, angle BAC = 60.0, then the length of BC is ()
================================================================================

STEP 1: COMPREHENSIVE IMAGE ANALYSIS
The image shows a circle with center O. Triangle ABC is inscribed in the circle. Angle BAC is marked as 60 degrees. A line segment from O is perpendicular to BC. The radius of the circle is labelled as 2. A small segment of the perpendicular from O to BC is labeled as 2.

STEP 2: CIRCLE-SPECIFIC ANALYSIS
Point O is the center of the circle. Points A, B, and C lie on the circle. OB and OC are radii of the circle. The length of the radius is 2. Line segment BC is a chord. The perpendicular from O to BC bisects BC.

STEP 3: ANGLE AND PERPENDICULARITY ANALYSIS
Angle BAC = 60 degrees. The line segment from O to BC is perpendicular to BC. Let D be the intersection of the perpendicular from O to BC. Angle ODB = 90 degrees. Since angle BAC is an inscribed angle, the central angle BOC = 2 * angle BAC = 2 * 60 = 120 degrees. Triangle OBC is an isosceles triangle because OB = OC = radius.

STEP 4: CONGRUENCE AND EQUALITY ANALYSIS
OB = OC (radii of the circle). Triangle OBC is an isosceles triangle. Angle OBC = Angle OCB.

STEP 5: INTERSECTION AND POSITIONING ANALYSIS
Let D be the point where the perpendicular from O intersects BC. D is the midpoint of BC.

STEP 6: CONSTRAINT AND RELATIONSHIP SYNTHESIS
Since the radius is 2, OB = OC = 2. In triangle OBC, angle BOC = 120 degrees. Angle OBC = Angle OCB = (180 - 120)/2 = 30 degrees. Also, OD is perpendicular to BC, and it bisects angle BOC. Thus, angle BOD = angle COD = 120/2 = 60 degrees. Triangle OBD is a right triangle.

STEP 7: QUESTION-DRIVEN COMPLETENESS CHECK
We need to find the length of BC. Since D is the midpoint of BC, BC = 2 * BD. We know OB = 2 and angle BOD = 60 degrees. In right triangle OBD, sin(60) = BD/OB, so BD = OB * sin(60) = 2 * sin(60) = 2 * (sqrt(3)/2) = sqrt(3). Therefore, BC = 2 * sqrt(3).
