Question: Circle I is the inscribed circle of triangle ABC, D, E, F are 3 tangent points, if angle DEF = 52.0, then the degree of angle A is ()

- Shapes: Triangle ABC, Circle I
- Points: A, B, C, D, E, F, I
- Lines: AB, BC, CA, DE, EF, FD, ID, IE, IF
- Angles: Angle DEF = 52 degrees
- Special markings: D, E, F are tangent points on the circle.

STEP 2: CIRCLE-SPECIFIC ANALYSIS
- Center: I
- Points on circle: D, E, F
- Radii: ID, IE, IF
- Tangents: AB, BC, CA are tangent to the circle at D, E, F respectively.
- ID is perpendicular to AB, IE is perpendicular to BC, IF is perpendicular to AC.
- ID = IE = IF

STEP 3: ANGLE AND PERPENDICULARITY ANALYSIS

- Angle IDE = Angle IED. Triangle IDE is isosceles.
- Angle IFE = Angle IEF. Triangle IFE is isosceles.
- Angle IDF = Angle IFD. Triangle IDF is isosceles.
- Angle ADI = Angle AFI = 90 degrees.
- Angle BEI = Angle BDI = 90 degrees.
- Angle CEI = Angle CFI = 90 degrees.

STEP 4: CONGRUENCE AND EQUALITY ANALYSIS
- ID = IE = IF (radii of the circle).
- Triangles ADI and AFI might be congruent.
- Triangles BDI and BEI might be congruent.
- Triangles CEI and CFI might be congruent.
- AD = AF, BD = BE, CE = CF

STEP 5: INTERSECTION AND POSITIONING ANALYSIS
- I is the incenter of triangle ABC.
- AB and AC intersect at A.
- AB and BC intersect at B.
- AC and BC intersect at C.
- DE, EF, FD are lines connecting the points of tangency.

STEP 6: CONSTRAINT AND RELATIONSHIP SYNTHESIS
- Since I is the incenter, AI, BI, CI are angle bisectors of angles A, B, C respectively.
- Relationships between angles formed by the inscribed circle and triangle ABC.
