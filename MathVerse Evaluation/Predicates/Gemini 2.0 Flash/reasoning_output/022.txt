STEP 3: ANGLE AND PERPENDICULARITY ANALYSIS
- Angle DAC is given as 28 degrees.
- In a rhombus, opposite angles are equal, and adjacent angles are supplementary.
- Diagonals of a rhombus bisect the angles.
- Since ABCD is a rhombus, Angle BAC = Angle DAC = 28 degrees.
- Angle ACB = Angle CAD = 28 degrees.
- Angle BCD = Angle BAD. Angle ABC = Angle ADC.
- Angle BCD + Angle ADC = 180 degrees. Angle ADC = 2 * (28) + Angle BOC.

STEP 4: CONGRUENCE AND EQUALITY ANALYSIS
- Since ABCD is a rhombus, AB = BC = CD = DA.
- AM = CN (given).
- Since AB = CD, then AB - AM = CD - CN, which implies MB = DN.
- AO = OC. Diagonals of a rhombus bisect each other.
- Triangles AMO and CNO are congruent (SAS congruence).

STEP 5: INTERSECTION AND POSITIONING ANALYSIS
- M lies on AB. N lies on CD. O lies on AC and MN.
- O is the intersection of AC and MN.

STEP 6: CONSTRAINT AND RELATIONSHIP SYNTHESIS
- Since ABCD is a rhombus, AB || CD, and AD || BC.
- Angle DAC = Angle BCA = 28.
- Since AM = CN and AB = CD, MB = ND.
- Angle AMO = Angle CNO (vertically opposite angles).

STEP 7: QUESTION-DRIVEN COMPLETENESS CHECK
