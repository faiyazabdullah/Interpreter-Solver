================================================================================
Question: A figure with a radius of $ 5 \mathrm{~mm}$. Find the exact area of the given figure:
================================================================================

STEP 1: COMPREHENSIVE IMAGE ANALYSIS
- Geometric shapes: Sector of a circle, right angle.
- Points: Implicit center of the circle.
- Lines: Two radii of the circle forming the sector.
- Measurements: Radius = 5 mm.
- Special markings: Right angle symbol at the center of the circle.

STEP 2: CIRCLE-SPECIFIC ANALYSIS
- Implied center: Let's call it O. Points on circle: Two points defining the radii forming the sector. Let's call them A and B.
- Radii: Line(O,A), Line(O,B)
- Central Angle: Angle(A,O,B)
- Radius length: 5 mm

STEP 3: ANGLE AND PERPENDICULARITY ANALYSIS
- Right angle: Angle(A,O,B) is a right angle.
- Measure of Angle(A,O,B) is 90 degrees.
- Perpendicular: Line(O,A) is perpendicular to Line(O,B).

STEP 4: CONGRUENCE AND EQUALITY ANALYSIS
- Equal radii: LengthOf(Line(O,A)) = LengthOf(Line(O,B)).

STEP 5: INTERSECTION AND POSITIONING ANALYSIS
- Lines Line(O,A) and Line(O,B) intersect at point O.

STEP 6: CONSTRAINT AND RELATIONSHIP SYNTHESIS
- The figure is a quarter of a circle (sector) because the central angle is 90 degrees.

STEP 7: QUESTION-DRIVEN COMPLETENESS CHECK
- The radius is given as 5 mm.
- The central angle is 90 degrees.
- The figure is a sector of a circle.
