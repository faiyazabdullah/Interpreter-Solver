Question: The height of the right pyramid is 7mm, the length of its base's side is 11mm. Find the exact volume of the right pyramid pictured.
================================================================================

STEP 1: COMPREHENSIVE IMAGE ANALYSIS
- Identify geometric shapes: pyramid, square.
- Identify points: We can imagine the pyramid having vertices A, B, C, D as the base and E as the apex. Let the center of the base be point O.
- Identify lines: Lines AB, BC, CD, DA are the sides of the square base. Lines EA, EB, EC, ED are the edges connecting the base to the apex. Line EO is the height of the pyramid.
- Note visible measurements: Height EO = 7mm, side of the base AB = BC = CD = DA = 11mm.
- Identify special markings: There is a right angle indicator between the base and the height line.
- Implied constructions: EO is perpendicular to the base at point O.

STEP 2: CIRCLE-SPECIFIC ANALYSIS (If circles are present)
Not applicable.

STEP 3: ANGLE AND PERPENDICULARITY ANALYSIS
Angle between the base and the height is 90 degrees. So, angle EOA, EOB, EOC and EOD are all right angles.

STEP 4: CONGRUENCE AND EQUALITY ANALYSIS
The base is a square, so AB = BC = CD = DA.

STEP 5: INTERSECTION AND POSITIONING ANALYSIS
Point O is at the center of the base square. Line EO intersects the base at point O.

STEP 6: CONSTRAINT AND RELATIONSHIP SYNTHESIS
The pyramid is a right pyramid with a square base.

STEP 7: QUESTION-DRIVEN COMPLETENESS CHECK
We have identified all necessary information to define the shape and dimensions of the pyramid.
