STEP 2: CIRCLE-SPECIFIC ANALYSIS
- There are no circles.

STEP 3: ANGLE AND PERPENDICULARITY ANALYSIS
- Angles: Angle VAW = theta
- Right angles: All angles at the base (ABCD) are right angles, angle VWD = 90 degrees.
- VW is perpendicular to plane ABCD. Therefore VW is perpendicular to AW, BW, CW, and DW.

STEP 4: CONGRUENCE AND EQUALITY ANALYSIS
- AB = BC = CD = DA = 12 cm
- W is the center of the square ABCD, thus AW = BW = CW = DW.
- Triangle VWD, triangle VWC, triangle VBA, and triangle VAD are not necessarily congruent.

STEP 5: INTERSECTION AND POSITIONING ANALYSIS
- W is the intersection of diagonals AC and BD.
- W is the center of the square.

STEP 6: CONSTRAINT AND RELATIONSHIP SYNTHESIS
- Pythagorean theorem can be applied to triangle VAW if the length of AW is determined.
- AW is half the length of the diagonal of the square base.
- The length of the diagonal of the square is 12*sqrt(2). Thus, AW = 6*sqrt(2)
