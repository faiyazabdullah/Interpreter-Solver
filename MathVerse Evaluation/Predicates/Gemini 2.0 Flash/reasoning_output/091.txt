STEP 1: COMPREHENSIVE IMAGE ANALYSIS
The image shows a semi-circle with diameter AB, center O, and points C and D on the arc. We have a quadrilateral ABCD inscribed in the semi-circle. Angle BAC is given as 30 degrees, and it is given that arc AD is equal to arc CD.

STEP 2: CIRCLE-SPECIFIC ANALYSIS
A, B, C, and D lie on the circle. AB is the diameter of circle O. OA and OB are radii.

STEP 3: ANGLE AND PERPENDICULARITY ANALYSIS
Angle BAC = 30 degrees. Since arc AD = arc CD, the inscribed angles subtended by these arcs are equal, therefore angle ABD = angle CBD. Angle ACB is a right angle because it is inscribed in a semi-circle and subtends the diameter AB.

STEP 4: CONGRUENCE AND EQUALITY ANALYSIS
arc AD = arc CD implies angle ABD = angle CBD and also implies chord AD = chord CD.

STEP 5: INTERSECTION AND POSITIONING ANALYSIS
Points C and D lie on the semi-circle.

STEP 6: CONSTRAINT AND RELATIONSHIP SYNTHESIS
Since angle ACB is a right angle and angle BAC is 30 degrees, angle ABC is 60 degrees. Since arc AD = arc CD, angle ABD = angle CBD, therefore angle ABD = 30 degrees.

STEP 7: QUESTION-DRIVEN COMPLETENESS CHECK
We need to determine angle DAC. Angle DAC = angle DBC since they subtend arc DC. Since arc AD = arc CD, angle ABD = angle CBD. Angle ABC = 60 degrees. Therefore, angle ABD = angle CBD = 30 degrees. Thus, angle DAC = angle DBC = 30 degrees.
