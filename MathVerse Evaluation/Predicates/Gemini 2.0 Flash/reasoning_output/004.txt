Question: As shown in the figure, AB is tangent to circle O at point B, and the extended line of AO intersects circle O at point C. Connect BC, if angle A = 36.0, then angle C is equal to ()
================================================================================

STEP 1: COMPREHENSIVE IMAGE ANALYSIS
- Geometric shapes: Circle, Triangle, Line segment.
- Points: A, B, C, O.
- Lines: AB, AO, BC, BO, CO.
- Measurements: Angle A = 36 degrees.
- Special markings: AB is tangent to circle O at point B.

STEP 2: CIRCLE-SPECIFIC ANALYSIS
- Center of the circle: O
- Points on the circle: B, C
- Radii: OB, OC
- Tangent: AB is tangent to circle O at point B.
- The relationship between the radius and the tangent: OB is perpendicular to AB.

STEP 3: ANGLE AND PERPENDICULARITY ANALYSIS
- Angle A = 36 degrees.
- Since AB is tangent to circle O at point B, Angle ABO = 90 degrees.

STEP 4: CONGRUENCE AND EQUALITY ANALYSIS
- OB = OC because both are radii of the circle.
- Therefore, triangle OBC is an isosceles triangle.

STEP 5: INTERSECTION AND POSITIONING ANALYSIS
- AO intersects circle O at point C.

STEP 6: CONSTRAINT AND RELATIONSHIP SYNTHESIS
- In triangle ABO, Angle A = 36 degrees, and Angle ABO = 90 degrees. Therefore, Angle AOB = 180 - 90 - 36 = 54 degrees.
- Since triangle OBC is isosceles with OB = OC, Angle OBC = Angle OCB. Also, Angle BOC = 2 * Angle BAC
- Since OC = OB, then Triangle(BOC) is Isosceles. Therefore Angle(OBC) = Angle(OCB). Also Angle(BOC) = 180 - 2 * Angle(OCB)
- Angle BOC and Angle AOB are supplementary, so they add to 180 degrees. Thus Angle BOC = 180 - Angle AOB = 180 - 54 = 126
