- Shapes: Circle, Triangles

- Lines: AB, CD, AC, AD, BC
- Angles: Angle ADC = 26 degrees
- AB is the diameter of circle O.
- O is the center of the circle.

STEP 2: CIRCLE-SPECIFIC ANALYSIS
- Center: O
- Points on the circle: A, B, C, D
- AB is the diameter.
- AC, AD, BC, CD are chords.
- OA = OB = radius of the circle.
- Angle ACB is an inscribed angle subtended by diameter AB, therefore it is a right angle.

STEP 3: ANGLE AND PERPENDICULARITY ANALYSIS
- Angle ADC = 26 degrees.
- Angle ACB = 90 degrees.

STEP 4: CONGRUENCE AND EQUALITY ANALYSIS
- OA = OB (radii of the same circle).

STEP 5: INTERSECTION AND POSITIONING ANALYSIS
- Lines AC and BC intersect at C.
- Lines AD and CD intersect at D.
- Line AB passes through O, the center of the circle.

STEP 6: CONSTRAINT AND RELATIONSHIP SYNTHESIS
- Since AB is a diameter, angle ACB = 90 degrees.
- Angles ADC and ABC subtend the same arc AC, hence they are equal. Angle ABC = 26 degrees.
- In triangle ABC, angle CAB + angle ABC + angle ACB = 180 degrees.
