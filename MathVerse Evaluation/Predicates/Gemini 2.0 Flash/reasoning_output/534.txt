Question: Below is the graph of a trigonometric function. It has a maximum point at $\left(\frac{1}{4} \pi,-2.2\right)$ and a minimum point at $\left(\frac{3}{4} \pi,-8.5\right)$. What is the amplitude of the function?
================================================================================

STEP 1: COMPREHENSIVE IMAGE ANALYSIS
- The image shows the graph of a trigonometric function.
- There are two points labeled on the graph: $(\frac{1}{4}\pi, -2.2)$ which is a maximum and $(\frac{3}{4}\pi, -8.5)$ which is a minimum.
- The y-axis ranges from -9 to 7.
- The x-axis ranges from -3 to 6.

STEP 2: AMPLITUDE CALCULATION
The amplitude of a trigonometric function is half the difference between its maximum and minimum values. In this case, the maximum value of the function is -2.2 and the minimum value is -8.5.
Amplitude = (max - min) / 2 = (-2.2 - (-8.5)) / 2 = (-2.2 + 8.5) / 2 = 6.3 / 2 = 3.15
