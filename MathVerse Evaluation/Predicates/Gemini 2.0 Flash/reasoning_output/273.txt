Question: Two triangles M Q N and M P O are similar with a shared vertex M. Q N and P O are parallel. M Q = 5, M N = 6, and Q P = x. Find Q P.
================================================================================

STEP 1: COMPREHENSIVE IMAGE ANALYSIS
The image shows two triangles, M Q N and M P O. They share a common vertex M. Line segment Q N is parallel to line segment P O. The following lengths are given: M Q = 5, M N = 6, N O = 3 3/5, and Q P = x.

STEP 2: CIRCLE-SPECIFIC ANALYSIS
There are no circles in the image.

STEP 3: ANGLE AND PERPENDICULARITY ANALYSIS
Since Q N is parallel to P O, Angle(M,Q,N) = Angle(M,P,O) and Angle(M,N,Q) = Angle(M,O,P).

STEP 4: CONGRUENCE AND EQUALITY ANALYSIS
Triangles M Q N and M P O are similar.

STEP 5: INTERSECTION AND POSITIONING ANALYSIS
Point Q lies on line M P, Point N lies on line M O, Lines Q N and P O are parallel.

STEP 6: CONSTRAINT AND RELATIONSHIP SYNTHESIS
Since triangles M Q N and M P O are similar, M Q / M P = M N / M O. We have M Q = 5, M N = 6, N O = 3 3/5 = 18/5. M O = M N + N O = 6 + 18/5 = 30/5 + 18/5 = 48/5. M P = M Q + Q P = 5 + x.
So, 5 / (5 + x) = 6 / (48/5) = 6 * 5 / 48 = 30 / 48 = 5/8
So, 5 / (5 + x) = 5/8
5 + x = 8
x = 3
