QUESTION: \overrightarrow B A and \overrightarrow B C are opposite rays and \overrightarrow B D bisects \angle A B F. If m \angle F B C = 2 x + 25 and m \angle A B F = 10 x - 1, find m \angle D B F.

REASONING:
================================================================================
### STEP 1: IMAGE ANALYSIS
- I can see a geometric figure with points A, B, C, D, and F.
- Lines \( \overrightarrow{BA} \) and \( \overrightarrow{BC} \) form an angle at point B.
- Lines \( \overrightarrow{BD} \) and \( \overrightarrow{BF} \) intersect at point B.
- \( \overrightarrow{BA} \) and \( \overrightarrow{BC} \) are opposite rays, indicating they form a straight line through point B.
- \( \overrightarrow{BD} \) bisects \( \angle ABF \).
- The measure of \( \angle FBC \) is given as \( 2x + 25 \).
- The measure of \( \angle ABF \) is given as \( 10x - 1 \).

### STEP 2: RELATIONSHIP IDENTIFICATION
- \( \overrightarrow{BA} \) and \( \overrightarrow{BC} \) are opposite rays forming a straight line through point B.
- \( \overrightarrow{BD} \) bisects \( \angle ABF \).
- Since \( \overrightarrow{BA} \) and \( \overrightarrow{BC} \) form a straight line, \( \angle ABC \) is a straight angle, which measures \( 180^\circ \).

### STEP 3: MEASUREMENT ANALYSIS
- Given: \( m\angle FBC = 2x + 25 \)
- Given: \( m\angle ABF = 10x - 1 \)
- Since \( \angle ABC = 180^\circ \), we have:
  \[
  m\angle ABF + m\angle FBC = 180^\circ
  \]

### STEP 4: QUESTION ANALYSIS
- The question asks us to find \( m\angle DBF \).
- Since \( \overrightarrow{BD} \) bisects \( \angle ABF \), \( m\angle DBF \) is half of \( m\angle ABF \).

### STEP 5: PREDICATE PLANNING
- Represent the straight line and the angle bisector.
- Use the given angle measures to find the required angle.
