Question: Find x. Round to the nearest tenth if necessary. Assume that segments that appear to be tangent are tangent.

STEP 1: IMAGE ANALYSIS
- I can see a circle with two intersecting chords, CD and BE, intersecting at point A.
- Length of DA is 5.
- Length of AB is x.
- Length of AE is 4.
- Length of AC is x+8.

STEP 2: RELATIONSHIP IDENTIFICATION
- The intersecting chords theorem states that for two chords intersecting within a circle, the product of the segments of one chord equals the product of the segments of the other chord.
- Therefore, (DA)(AB) = (AC)(AE).

STEP 3: MEASUREMENT ANALYSIS
- DA = 5
- AB = x
- AE = 4
- AC = x+8

STEP 4: QUESTION ALIGNMENT
- The question asks for the value of x.
- Using the intersecting chords theorem we can set up an equation to solve for x.

STEP 5: PREDICATE PLANNING
- Represent the circle and the chords.
- Represent the given lengths.
- Represent the relationship from the intersecting chords theorem.
