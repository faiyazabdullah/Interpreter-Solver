#### STEP 1: IMAGE ANALYSIS
- **Geometric Shapes**: The figure is a parallelogram labeled \(ABCD\).
- **Points**: Four vertices are labeled as \(A\), \(B\), \(C\), and \(D\).
- **Lines**: Sides of the parallelogram are \(AB\), \(BC\), \(CD\), and \(DA\).
- **Measurements**:
  - Side \(AB = 20\) ft.
  - Side \(AD = 18\) ft.

#### STEP 2: RELATIONSHIP IDENTIFICATION
- **Parallelogram Properties**:
  - Opposite sides are equal: \(AB = CD\) and \(AD = BC\).
  - Opposite sides are parallel: \(AB \parallel CD\) and \(AD \parallel BC\).
- **Height**: The height from \(C\) to \(AD\) is perpendicular to \(AD\), confirming the standard definition of height in a parallelogram.

#### STEP 3: MEASUREMENT ANALYSIS
- **Extracted Measurements**:
  - \(AB = 20\) ft.
  - \(AD = 18\) ft.
  - Height = \(16\) ft (not directly used for perimeter but confirms the shape's structure).
- **Relevant Measurements**:
  - The lengths of two adjacent sides (\(AB\) and \(AD\)) are sufficient to determine the perimeter since opposite sides of a parallelogram are equal.

#### STEP 4: QUESTION ANALYSIS
- The question asks for the **perimeter** of the parallelogram.
- The perimeter of a parallelogram is given by:
  \[
  \text{Perimeter} = 2 \times (\text{Length of one pair of opposite sides} + \text{Length of the other pair of opposite sides})
  \]
- Using the given measurements:
  - \(AB = CD = 20\) ft.
  - \(AD = BC = 18\) ft.
- Therefore, the perimeter can be calculated as:
  \[
  \text{Perimeter} = 2 \times (AB + AD) = 2 \times (20 + 18) = 2 \times 38 = 76 \text{ ft}.
  \]
