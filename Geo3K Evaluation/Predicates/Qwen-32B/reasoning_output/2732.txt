================================================================================
QUESTION: A square is inscribed in a circle having a radius of 6 inches. Find the length of the side of the square.

REASONING:
================================================================================
### STEP-BY-STEP REASONING

#### STEP 1: IMAGE ANALYSIS
- **Geometric Shapes and Points**: 
  - A square \(ABCD\) is inscribed in a circle.
  - The center of the circle is point \(E\).
  - The circle has a radius of 6 inches.
  - The square's vertices \(A\), \(B\), \(C\), and \(D\) lie on the circumference of the circle.

- **Lines and Segments**:
  - The sides of the square are \(AB\), \(BC\), \(CD\), and \(DA\).
  - The diagonals of the square are \(AC\) and \(BD\), which pass through the center \(E\).

- **Measurements**:
  - The radius of the circle is given as 6 inches.

- **Special Markings**:
  - The square is inscribed, meaning its vertices touch the circle.
  - The diagonals of the square are diameters of the circle.

#### STEP 2: RELATIONSHIP IDENTIFICATION
- **Square Properties**:
  - All sides of the square are equal.
  - The diagonals of the square are equal and bisect each other at right angles.
  - The diagonals are also the diameters of the circumscribing circle.

- **Circle Properties**:
  - The radius of the circle is 6 inches.
  - The center of the circle is the intersection of the diagonals of the square.
  - The diagonals of the square are twice the radius of the circle.

- **Relationships**:
  - The diagonals of the square are equal and each is a diameter of the circle.
  - The length of each diagonal is \(2 \times \text{radius} = 2 \times 6 = 12\) inches.
  - The relationship between the side length \(s\) of the square and its diagonal \(d\) is given by the Pythagorean theorem: \(d = s\sqrt{2}\).

#### STEP 3: MEASUREMENT ANALYSIS
- **Given Measurement**:
  - Radius of the circle = 6 inches.
  - Diagonal of the square = Diameter of the circle = \(2 \times 6 = 12\) inches.

- **Derived Measurements**:
  - Let the side length of the square be \(s\).
  - Using the relationship \(d = s\sqrt{2}\), where \(d = 12\) inches:
    \[
    12 = s\sqrt{2}
    \]
    Solving for \(s\):
    \[
    s = \frac{12}{\sqrt{2}} = 6\sqrt{2} \text{ inches}.
    \]

#### STEP 4: QUESTION ANALYSIS
- The question asks for the length of the side of the square.
- The relevant geometric property is the relationship between the side length of the square and its diagonal.
- The diagonal of the square is equal to the diameter of the circle, which is \(12\) inches.
- Using the formula \(d = s\sqrt{2}\), we can solve for \(s\).

#### STEP 5: PREDICATE PLANNING
- We need to represent the square, the circle, the relationship between the square and the circle, and the measurements.
- Predicates should capture the inscribed nature of the square, the radius of the circle, and the relationship between the diagonal of the square and its side length.
