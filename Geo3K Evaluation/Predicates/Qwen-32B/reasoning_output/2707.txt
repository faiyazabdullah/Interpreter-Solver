- **Derived Measurements**:

    \[
    CE = \sqrt{BC^2 - BE^2} = \sqrt{15^2 - 5^2} = \sqrt{225 - 25} = \sqrt{200} = 10\sqrt{2} \text{ inches}.
    \]

#### STEP 4: QUESTION ANALYSIS
- The question asks for the area of the parallelogram.
- The formula for the area of a parallelogram is:
  \[
  \text{Area} = \text{base} \times \text{height}.
  \]
- Here, the base \(AB = 13\) inches, and the height \(CE = 10\sqrt{2}\) inches.
- The area is:
  \[
  \text{Area} = 13 \times 10\sqrt{2} = 130\sqrt{2}.
  \]
- Approximating \(\sqrt{2} \approx 1.414\):
  \[
  \text{Area} \approx 130 \times 1.414 = 183.82 \text{ square inches}.
  \]
- Rounding to the nearest tenth:
  \[
  \text{Area} \approx 183.8 \text{ square inches}.
  \]
